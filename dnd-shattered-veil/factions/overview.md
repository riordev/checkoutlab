# Factions of The Shattered Veil

## Overview

In a world fractured by planar catastrophe, power doesn't just flow from thrones and temples. It comes from those who can offer safety, knowledge, or transformation. The factions of the Shattered Veil represent competing visions of survival—and competing answers to the question of what the world should become.

This section details six major powers, their goals, their secrets, and how they might interact with player characters.

---

## Quick Reference

| Faction | Core Goal | Alignment | Relationship to PCs |
|---------|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| **The Threshold Concordat** | Unify all Threshold Cities under centralized control | Lawful Neutral | Powerful ally or oppressive enemy |
| **The Veil-Walkers** | Preserve freedom of movement between cities | Chaotic Good | Guides, allies, potential mentors |
| **The Restoration Cults** | "Heal" the Veil through spiritual/magical means | Lawful Good to Lawful Evil | Zealous allies or dangerous fanatics |
| **The Bleeding Court** | Claim the Material Plane as Feywild territory | Chaotic Neutral | Beautiful, dangerous, unpredictable |
| **The Forge-Bound** | Harness planar energy for creation | Neutral | Source of powerful items, amoral |
| **The Cartographers' Conspiracy** | Understand and potentially heal the Shattering | Neutral Good | Secretive allies with hidden knowledge |

## The Threshold Concordat

### Overview
An alliance of the five largest Threshold Cities (Aethertide, Ironhold, Skyreach, Duskmere, and Dawnport), the Concordat represents the closest thing to a world government in the Shattered Age. They maintain the Roads, regulate trade, and present a united front against threats.

Underneath this cooperation lies a simple truth: **Aethertide dominates.** The other cities are increasingly vassals to the Magisters' vision of order and control.

### Public Goals
- Maintain safe passage between Threshold Cities
- Regulate magic to prevent further planar damage
- Coordinate defense against major threats
- Eventually find a way to repair the Veil

### Secret Goals
- Consolidate all Threshold Cities under Aethertide's direct control
- Eliminate independent settlements that refuse Concordat authority
- Control all knowledge about the Shattering to prevent panic
- Some leaders believe the Veil cannot be repaired—and are preparing for permanent stratification

### Leadership
**The Concordat Council** meets quarterly in rotating cities. Each city sends three representatives:

From **Aethertide:**
- **Magister Thaddeus** (official leader, Evocation)
- **Ambassador Vex** (diplomatic, actually a spy)
- **Lord Protector Crane** (military, believes in the Concordat's ideals)

From other cities (increasingly powerless):
- Representatives from Ironhold, Skyreach, Duskmere, and Dawnport who vote as Aethertide tells them

### Resources
- **The Stabilizer Corps:** Elite law enforcement/military drawn from all member cities
- **The Roads:** Network of relatively safe paths maintained by Concordat engineers
- **Essence Reserves:** Massive stockpiles of planar energy, giving them economic dominance
- **Information Network:** Spies in every major settlement

### Relationships
| Faction | Relationship | Notes |
|---------|--------------|-------|
| Veil-Walkers | Hostile | View them as smugglers and security risks |
| Restoration Cults | Tolerant | Useful for controlling populations, dangerous if too powerful |
| Bleeding Court | War | Active conflict over territory |
| Forge-Bound | Mercantile | Buy their services, watch them carefully |
| Cartographers' Conspiracy | Unknown | Don't know they exist |

### How PCs Get Involved
- **As Agents:** Recruited to enforce Concordat law, investigate threats, or represent Concordat interests
- **As Enemies:** Fighting against Concordat oppression, helping smugglers, defending independent settlements
- **As Mercenaries:** Hired for deniable operations the Concordat can't be seen conducting

### Adventure Hooks
- Track down Veil-Walker smugglers who've stolen Concordat secrets
- Investigate why a Threshold City's Stability Field is weakening
- Protect a Concordat diplomat during negotiations with Thornhaven
- Discover evidence that the Concordat knew the Stability Engines were flawed from the start

## The Veil-Walkers

### Overview
Wandering mystics, guides, and messengers who've learned to survive in the Fractured Wilds. They reject Threshold City authority, believing that freedom of movement is essential to human dignity—and that the Concordat's "safety" is just another prison.

Veil-Walkers are recognizable by their **prismatic cloaks**, dyed with pigments extracted from planar-touched plants that shift color based on nearby magical activity. They carry **Wayfinders**—compass-like devices that point toward the nearest ley line or stable path.

### Public Goals
- Maintain communication between all settlements, not just Threshold Cities
- Protect travelers through the Fractured Wilds
- Preserve knowledge of the old roads and discover new ones
- Resist Concordat attempts to monopolize travel

### Secret Goals
- Map the entire Shattered world, including regions the Concordat wants kept hidden
- Develop techniques for surviving without Stability Fields
- Find a way to "ride" planar bleeding rather than just survive it
- Some believe the Veil should be torn completely—only then can true freedom exist

### Leadership
The Veil-Walkers have no formal hierarchy, but certain individuals command immense respect:

**Elias Vance** ("The Warden of the Threshold")
- The closest thing to a leader, though he'd reject the title
- Has walked every Road and countless uncharted paths
- Carries a map of the Shattered world tattooed on his skin
- Secretly dying of planar poisoning, racing to train a successor

**The Pathfinders**
- Seven master navigators who've mapped specific regions
- Each trains apprentices and maintains their section of the collective map
- Named after their territories: Marsh, Desert, Mountain, Forest, Urban, Sea, and Void

### Resources
- **The Hidden Roads:** Secret paths unknown to the Concordat
- **Wayfinder Crafting:** Knowledge of how to make devices that navigate planar instability
- **Safe Houses:** Network of hidden shelters throughout the Fractured Wilds
- **Information:** They know things about the Shattering that the Concordat has suppressed

### Relationships
| Faction | Relationship | Notes |
|---------|--------------|-------|
| Threshold Concordat | Hostile | Constantly hunted as criminals |
| Restoration Cults | Cautious | Sometimes allies against Concordat, sometimes targets of cult purges |
| Bleeding Court | Complex | Some deals, some betrayals, no trust |
| Forge-Bound | Friendly | Mutual respect, trade equipment for protection |
| Cartographers' Conspiracy | Allied | Share information, different methods |

### How PCs Get Involved
- **As Apprentices:** Learning the paths from a master Veil-Walker
- **As Clients:** Hiring a guide for dangerous travel
- **As Sympathizers:** Helping smuggle people or information between cities
- **As Hunters:** Working for the Concordat to capture Veil-Walkers

### Adventure Hooks
- Escort a caravan through a newly-dangerous section of the Wilds
- Rescue a captured Pathfinder before they break under Concordat interrogation
- Discover a Hidden Road that leads somewhere impossible
- Find out why Veil-Walkers are disappearing near The Maw

## The Restoration Cults

### Overview
Religious movements devoted to "healing" the Veil. They emerged in the early years after the Shattering, when desperate people sought meaning in the catastrophe. Over three centuries, they've fragmented into dozens of sects with competing theologies and methods.

What unites them is the core belief: **The Shattering was not inevitable. The Veil can be restored. And someone must atone for the sin that broke the world.**

### Major Sects

#### The Purifiers (Lawful Good)
Believe the Veil tore because mortals became too dependent on magic. They seek to restore it through prayer, asceticism, and the rejection of arcane power.
- **Methods:** Meditation, purification rituals, destroying magical items
- **Leader:** **Saint Alia**, a former archmage who renounced magic
- **Attitude to PCs:** Supportive if they avoid magic, concerned if they use it

#### The Sacrifice Doctrine (Lawful Evil)
Believe blood is the only currency that can mend a wound in reality. They conduct ritual sacrifices, targeting the "most magically corrupted"—often planar-touched individuals.
- **Methods:** Human sacrifice, blood magic, hunting the touched
- **Leader:** **The Red Shepherd**, identity unknown
- **Attitude to PCs:** Dangerous enemies if planar-touched, potential converts if "pure"

#### The Harmonists (Neutral Good)
Believe the Veil can be healed through proper ritual and the cooperation of all peoples. They seek to reconstruct the Grand Unification ritual—correctly, this time.
- **Methods:** Research, diplomacy, ritual magic
- **Leader:** **Archivist Merek**, who has studied the Shattering for 200 years
- **Attitude to PCs:** Potential allies, sources of information, recipients of aid

#### The Accelerationists (Chaotic Evil)
Believe the Veil isn't broken enough. They perform rituals to widen planar rifts, thinking that total collapse will birth a new, better reality.
- **Methods:** Sabotage, ritual desecration, opening portals
- **Leader:** **The Open Hand**, a cult of personality around a charismatic speaker
- **Attitude to PCs:** Enemies of everything, including themselves

### Public Goals (Varies by Sect)
- Restore the Veil through spiritual/magical means
- Prepare mortals for the restored world
- Atone for the sin of the Shattering

### Secret Goals
- Many sects are actually controlled by other powers (the Echoes, the Bleeding Court, even the Concordat)
- The Harmonists' ritual research has discovered something terrifying about what "healing" the Veil would actually require
- The Sacrifice Doctrine's leaders don't actually believe—they just enjoy the power

### Relationships
| Faction | Relationship | Notes |
|---------|--------------|-------|
| Threshold Concordat | Tolerant Hostility | Cults are useful for social control but dangerous if too powerful |
| Veil-Walkers | Varies | Some alliance against Concordat, some sects hunt Walkers as "corrupt" |
| Bleeding Court | Hidden Alliance | Some cults secretly serve fey interests |
| Forge-Bound | Hostile | View their creations as blasphemous |
| Cartographers' Conspiracy | Curious | Want access to their research |

### How PCs Get Involved
- **As Believers:** Joining a sect, advancing its goals
- **As Investigators:** Uncovering which cults are fronts for other powers
- **As Protectors:** Defending planar-touched individuals from the Sacrifice Doctrine
- **As Skeptics:** Investigating whether "healing" the Veil is even possible—or desirable

### Adventure Hooks
- Stop a Sacrifice Doctrine cell from burning a village of planar-touched
- Help the Harmonists recover pre-Shattering ritual texts
- Investigate a Purifier monastery where something ancient has been unearthed
- Infiltrate an Accelerationist cell planning to attack a Threshold City

## The Bleeding Court

### Overview
When the Veil tore, the Feywild bled through more completely than any other plane. The **Bleeding Court** is the result—fey nobles who've established permanent domains in the Material Plane, claiming the Shattered world as their rightful territory.

They are beautiful, cruel, capricious, and **ancient.** Most were old when mortal civilization was young. They see the Shattering not as a tragedy but as an **invitation**—the mortal world has finally become interesting enough to play in.

### Structure
The Court is divided into **Seasonal Houses**, each led by a sovereign:

**The House of Eternal Spring** (Lady Velorie)
- Domains of perpetual growth, beauty, and desire
- Collects mortals as lovers, servants, and decorations
- Most "diplomatic" with mortal powers

**The House of Endless Summer** (Lord Solanis)
- Domains of passion, conflict, and glory
- Incites wars for entertainment, recruits mortal champions
- Views the Crucible as competition

**The House of Undying Autumn** (The Duke of Thorns)
- Domains of decay, transformation, and nostalgia
- Preserves memories, hoards secrets, collects regrets
- Allied with Thornhaven through shared interest in natural cycles

**The House of Unyielding Winter** (The Snow Queen)
- Domains of silence, preservation, and endings
- Least active in mortal affairs, most dangerous when roused
- May have caused the last ice age

### Public Goals
- Establish permanent Feywild territories in the Material Plane
- "Educate" mortals in the proper ways of courtesy, obligation, and whimsy
- Maintain the "Game"—their eternal competition for status and amusement

### Secret Goals
- Merge the Feywild and Material Planes completely
- Transform all mortals into fey servants
- Find the source of the Shattering and claim it for themselves
- Some sovereigns want to heal the Veil; others want to tear it further

### Resources
- **Glamour:** Fey magic of illusion, enchantment, and transformation
- **The Wild Hunt:** An army of fey knights who enforce Court will
- **Bargains:** They can offer almost anything—for the right price
- **Time:** They have lived for millennia and can afford to be patient

### Relationships
| Faction | Relationship | Notes |
|---------|--------------|-------|
| Threshold Concordat | War | Active conflict over territory |
| Veil-Walkers | Complex | Sometimes allies, sometimes prey |
| Restoration Cults | Manipulation | Secretly control some cults |
| Forge-Bound | Commerce | Trade beauty and magic for artifacts |
| Cartographers' Conspiracy | Curious | Find them amusing but potentially dangerous |

### The Rules of Engagement
Dealing with the Bleeding Court requires understanding their peculiar ethics:

1. **Hospitality is Sacred:** Once accepted as a guest, you are safe. Until you leave.
2. **Gifts Create Obligation:** Accepting anything binds you to reciprocate.
3. **Names Have Power:** Never give your true name. Never ask for theirs.
4. **Courtesy is Armor:** Rudeness can be punished with death. Politeness can save your life.
5. **The Game is Everything:** They don't want to win—they want to play. Forever.

### How PCs Get Involved
- **As Pawns:** Drawn into Court politics, used as pieces in their games
- **As Champions:** Recruited to fight for a sovereign's honor
- **As Bargainers:** Seeking something only the fey can provide
- **As Resistors:** Defending mortal settlements from fey incursion

### Adventure Hooks
- Retrieve a mortal who accepted a fey bargain and now regrets it
- Negotiate peace between a Threshold City and a Summer House war band
- Compete in a fey tournament where the stakes are your memories
- Discover why the Snow Queen has begun awakening from her eternal slumber

## The Forge-Bound

### Overview
The Shattering didn't just break the world—it revealed new possibilities. The **Forge-Bound** are artificers, craftspeople, and inventors who've learned to harness planar energy directly, creating items and effects impossible before the Veil tore.

They have no unified organization, just a shared philosophy: **Reality is material. Magic is technique. Anything can be built.**

### Philosophy
The Forge-Bound believe that planar bleeding isn't a curse—it's a **resource.** Like fire, it can destroy or create depending on how it's handled. Their goal is mastery through understanding.

They reject moral judgments about their work. If something can be built, it should be. If someone will pay for it, it will be sold. Questions of "should" are for philosophers and priests.

### Major Groups

**The Crucible Forges** (The Crucible)
- Masters of elemental binding and heat-based crafting
- Create weapons, armor, and constructs of unparalleled quality
- Most traditional and respected

**The Gilded Cages** (Aethertide, underground)
- Specialists in binding and controlling magical entities
- Create "essence batteries," bound elementals, and (rumored) imprisoned souls
- Most feared and secretive

**The Verdant Works** (Thornhaven)
- Merge artifice with organic growth
- Create living machines, bio-tools, and symbiotic enhancements
- Most alien in their outlook

**The Chaos Forges** (Scattered, mobile)
- Experimentalists who embrace planar instability
- Create items of immense power and unpredictable function
- Most dangerous—to themselves and others

### Public Goals
- Push the boundaries of what's possible with planar technology
- Profit from their creations
- Train new generations of artificers

### Secret Goals
- Some seek to construct an artificial Stability Field that doesn't require oppression
- Others want to build a device that can manipulate the Veil itself
- A few believe they can recreate the Grand Unification ritual and finish what the archmages started

### Resources
- **Planar-Forged Items:** Weapons, armor, and tools of unprecedented power
- **Essence Extraction:** Techniques for harvesting planar energy
- **Knowledge:** Ancient texts, experimental data, trade secrets
- **Wealth:** Their creations command astronomical prices

### Relationships
| Faction | Relationship | Notes |
|---------|--------------|-------|
| Threshold Concordat | Mercantile | They buy, but the Concordat watches closely |
| Veil-Walkers | Friendly | Respect for self-reliance, trade equipment |
| Restoration Cults | Hostile | View their work as blasphemous |
| Bleeding Court | Commerce | Trade beauty and magic for artifacts |
| Cartographers' Conspiracy | Allied | Share research, different focuses |

### The Cost of Creation
Forge-Bound items are powerful but often come with side effects:

- **Essence Addiction:** Users may become dependent on planar-infused items
- **Planar Resonance:** Items can attract unwanted attention from other planes
- **Instability:** Chaos Forge items have unpredictable activation requirements
- **Moral Corruption:** Gilded Cage items may be powered by suffering

### How PCs Get Involved
- **As Customers:** Seeking specific items or upgrades
- **As Testers:** Paid to field-test experimental equipment
- **As Apprentices:** Learning the craft
- **As Investigators:** Uncovering the true cost of Forge-Bound creations

### Adventure Hooks
- Recover stolen Forge-Bound plans before they can be misused
- Test a new weapon and discover its horrifying true function
- Investigate why Forge-Bound items are spontaneously activating
- Negotiate between rival Forge-Bound factions competing for a rare resource

## The Cartographers' Conspiracy

### Overview
A secret society dedicated to **understanding** the Shattering—not to exploit it, not to heal it, but to truly **comprehend** what happened and what it means. They believe knowledge is the only path to survival, and that some truths must be kept secret until the world is ready.

The Conspiracy has existed since Year 1 of the Shattered Age. Its founders were students of the seven archmages who survived the ritual. They've been collecting information, conducting experiments, and preparing for... something.

### Public Goals
They have none. They don't exist, officially.

### Secret Goals
- Complete the **Grand Map**—a comprehensive understanding of the Shattered Veil
- Determine whether the Veil can (or should) be repaired
- Prepare for a predicted event called **"The Final Fracture"**
- Protect knowledge that could destroy the world if misused

### Organization
The Conspiracy is organized in **Cells**—small groups that don't know about each other, connected only through coded messages and dead drops. This structure protects the whole if any part is compromised.

**Ranks:**
- **Observers:** Information gatherers, usually embedded in other factions
- **Scribes:** Researchers who compile and analyze data
- **Navigators:** Field agents who investigate dangerous phenomena
- **Keepers:** Senior members who decide what knowledge to share and what to suppress

### Known Members
Very few are publicly known. Those who've been identified usually die soon after.

**The Cartographer** (title, not name)
- The overall leader, identity unknown even to most members
- Has been alive since the Shattering—or multiple people have held the title
- Communicates only through coded maps

**Sister Mira** (confirmed member, deceased)
- A nun who catalogued planar phenomena for 50 years
- Killed by Concordat agents after revealing the location of a "stable rift"
- Her research was never recovered

**Vex** (suspected member)
- Navigator Guildmaster of Sunderpoint
- Has access to information he shouldn't, asks questions he shouldn't
- If he is a member, he plays a dangerous double game

### Resources
- **The Grand Library:** Scattered across multiple hidden locations, containing the most complete records of the Shattered Age
- **The Map:** A constantly updated understanding of planar topology
- **Safe Houses:** Hidden locations where members can retreat
- **Influence:** Members embedded in every major faction

### Relationships
| Faction | Relationship | Notes |
|---------|--------------|-------|
| Threshold Concordat | Hidden Hostility | The Concordat hunts them; they know too much |
| Veil-Walkers | Allied | Share goals, different methods |
| Restoration Cults | Cautious | Want access to their research |
| Bleeding Court | Curious | Find them amusing but potentially dangerous |
| Forge-Bound | Allied | Share research, different focuses |

### The Final Fracture
The Conspiracy's greatest secret: they've predicted another event, worse than the Shattering. They call it **The Final Fracture.**

According to their calculations, the Veil is still tearing. The current instability is temporary—a prelude to total planar collapse. When the Final Fracture comes, the barriers between all planes will dissolve completely.

They disagree on when (estimates range from 5 to 500 years) and what to do about it. Some want to warn the world. Others believe panic would only make things worse. A radical faction thinks the only solution is to cause a controlled Fracture now, while they can direct it.

### How PCs Get Involved
- **As Recruits:** Discovered and recruited for their unique skills
- **As Investigators:** Uncovering the Conspiracy's existence and goals
- **As Assets:** Used for missions without knowing who they serve
- **As Threats:** The Conspiracy may target PCs who learn too much

### Adventure Hooks
- Decode a mysterious map that leads to a hidden Conspiracy library
- Protect a Navigator who knows about the Final Fracture from Concordat assassins
- Investigate a series of "accidents" targeting planar researchers
- Choose whether to reveal the Final Fracture prediction to the world

---

*In the Shattered Veil, no faction has all the answers. The question is: whose questions are you willing to help answer?*

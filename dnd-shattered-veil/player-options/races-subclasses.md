# Player Options: The Shattered Veil

## Overview

The Shattered Veil changes everything—including what it means to be an adventurer. The following options allow players to embody the unique realities of this broken world.

---

## Planar-Touched Races

### The Veil-Touched
When the Veil tore, some unborn children were exposed to raw planar energy. They emerged... different. These **Veil-Touched** appear mostly humanoid but bear signs of their exposure—glowing eyes, crystalline growths, or shadows that move independently.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Charisma score increases by 2, and one other ability score of your choice increases by 1.

**Age:** Veil-Touched mature at the same rate as their base species but live 50% longer.

**Alignment:** Tend toward chaos, but any alignment is possible.

**Size:** Medium or Small (choose one).

**Speed:** 30 feet.

**Planar Resonance:** You have resistance to one damage type (choose from fire, cold, lightning, or psychic). This choice cannot be changed.

**Veil Sight:** You can see normally in darkness, both magical and nonmagical, to a distance of 60 feet. When you do so, your eyes glow with faint colored light.

**Unstable Gift:** You know one cantrip from the sorcerer spell list. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for it. When you cast this cantrip, roll a d20. On a 1, a random minor magical effect occurs (DM's choice or roll on Wild Magic table).

**Planar Adaptation:** You have advantage on saving throws against effects that would move you to another plane against your will.

**Languages:** Common and one other language of your choice.

---

### Echo Born
Not everyone who died during the Shattering stayed dead. **Echo Born** are souls who returned from the afterlife—sometimes days, sometimes centuries after their death. They remember their past lives but also carry fragments of the planes they passed through.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Wisdom score increases by 2, and your Constitution score increases by 1.

**Age:** Echo Born don't age physically but may fade after 100-200 years of returned life.

**Alignment:** Often neutral, as their time beyond the Veil changed their perspective.

**Size:** Medium.

**Speed:** 30 feet.

**Deathless Nature:** You have advantage on death saving throws. When you stabilize at 0 hit points, you regain 1 hit point.

**Memory Fragments:** You have proficiency in one skill of your choice. This represents knowledge from your past life.

**Planar Echo:** When you finish a long rest, choose one damage type. Until your next long rest, you have resistance to that damage type. You can choose a different type each rest.

**Echo of the Self:** You can cast *misty step* once without expending a spell slot. Once you do so, you can't do so again until you finish a long rest. When you use this ability, you briefly appear translucent.

**Languages:** Common and two other languages (from your past life).

---

### Fey-Blooded
The Feywild's bleeding into the Material Plane has created a new generation of hybrids—those with fey ancestry from the Shattering itself. They combine mortal determination with fey caprice.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Dexterity score increases by 2, and your Charisma score increases by 1.

**Age:** Fey-Blooded mature slowly (reaching adulthood at 30) and can live up to 500 years.

**Alignment:** Strongly tend toward chaotic alignments.

**Size:** Medium.

**Speed:** 30 feet.

**Fey Ancestry:** You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed, and magic can't put you to sleep.

**Wild Step:** When you take the Dash action, difficult terrain doesn't cost you extra movement until the end of your turn.

**Glamour:** You know the *minor illusion* cantrip. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for it.

**Fey Charm:** You can cast *charm person* once without expending a spell slot. Once you do so, you can't do so again until you finish a long rest.

**Trance:** Fey-Blooded don't sleep. Instead, they meditate deeply for 4 hours a day.

**Languages:** Common and Sylvan.

---

### The Rootbound
Those born in Thornhaven or similar planar-hybrid environments sometimes develop symbiotic relationships with the living world around them. They are part plant, part fungal, entirely unique.

**Ability Score Increase:** Your Constitution score increases by 2, and your Wisdom score increases by 1.

**Age:** Rootbound mature at 20 and can live up to 300 years.

**Alignment:** Usually neutral good, following the balance of nature.

**Size:** Medium.

**Speed:** 30 feet.

**Living Anatomy:** You don't need to eat or breathe as long as you spend at least 4 hours in sunlight or moonlight each day. You are immune to ingested poisons but vulnerable to necrotic damage.

**Regrowth:** When you expend Hit Dice during a short rest to regain hit points, you regain an extra 1d4 hit points per die spent.

**Spore Cloud:** When you take damage, you can use your reaction to release spores. Each creature within 5 feet must succeed on a Constitution saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Con modifier) or be poisoned until the start of your next turn. Once you use this trait, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

**Photosynthesis:** If you spend at least 1 hour in direct sunlight, you don't need to eat for the next 24 hours.

**Languages:** Common and one other language.

## Subclasses

### Barbarian: Path of the Fractured Rage

Some barbarians don't just enter a rage—they let the planar chaos flow through them. In combat, they become conduits for the same forces that broke the world.

**Planar Fury**
Starting when you choose this path at 3rd level, when you enter a rage, choose one damage type: fire, cold, lightning, thunder, or acid. While raging, your melee weapon attacks deal an extra 1d6 damage of the chosen type. This increases to 1d8 at 10th level and 1d10 at 14th level.

**Unstable Resilience**
At 6th level, your exposure to planar energy has changed your body. You have resistance to one damage type (choose when you gain this feature). Each time you finish a long rest, you can change this resistance to a different type.

**Reality Tear**
At 10th level, when you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack while raging, you can open a momentary rift. The target takes an extra 3d10 force damage and must succeed on a Constitution saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Con modifier) or be stunned until the end of your next turn. Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

**Avatar of Chaos**
At 14th level, you become a living embodiment of planar fury. While raging, you emit an aura in a 10-foot radius. Hostile creatures that start their turn in the aura or enter it for the first time on their turn take damage equal to your Constitution modifier (minimum 1) of a type you choose when you enter your rage.

### Cleric: Domain of the Veil

Gods may have been distant during the Shattering, but the Veil itself became a source of divine power. Clerics of the Veil Domain draw power from the barrier between planes—whether to mend it, exploit it, or understand it.

**Domain Spells**
| Cleric Level | Spells |
|--------------|--------|
| 1st | *detect magic, shield* |
| 3rd | *misty step, see invisibility* |
| 5th | *counterspell, dispel magic* |
| 7th | *banishment, dimension door* |
| 9th | *planar binding, wall of force* |

**Bonus Proficiency**
When you choose this domain at 1st level, you gain proficiency with heavy armor.

**Channel Divinity: Stabilize**
Starting at 2nd level, you can use your Channel Divinity to momentarily reinforce the Veil. As an action, you create a 30-foot radius sphere centered on yourself where planar effects are suppressed. Within this area:
- Wild magic doesn't trigger
- Planar bleeding is temporarily halted
- Creatures have advantage on saving throws against planar effects

This effect lasts for 1 minute or until you lose concentration (as if concentrating on a spell).

**Planar Ward**
At 6th level, you can use your Channel Divinity to protect a creature from planar travel. For 1 minute, the target cannot be teleported or moved to another plane against their will. They also have advantage on saving throws against spells and effects that would charm or possess them.

**Divine Strike**
At 8th level, you gain the ability to infuse your weapon strikes with planar energy. Once on each of your turns when you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can cause the attack to deal an extra 1d8 force damage to the target. When you reach 14th level, the extra damage increases to 2d8.

**Master of Barriers**
At 17th level, you can cast *plane shift* once without expending a spell slot. When you do so, you can choose up to five willing creatures within 30 feet to travel with you. Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

### Druid: Circle of the Thornbloom

Druids of the Thornbloom have learned to work with the planar-hybrid life that emerged after the Shattering. They see the Thornbloom not as a corruption of nature but as its evolution.

**Thornbloom Companion**
Starting when you choose this circle at 2nd level, you gain a symbiotic relationship with the Thornbloom. As a bonus action, you can summon thorny vines to coat your body for 1 minute. While coated:
- You gain temporary hit points equal to your druid level + your Wisdom modifier
- Creatures that hit you with a melee attack take piercing damage equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1)
- Your melee weapon attacks deal an extra 1d6 poison damage

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

**Spore Magic**
At 2nd level, you learn to manipulate the spores of the Thornbloom. You can cast *fog cloud* without expending a spell slot. When you do so using this feature, the cloud is composed of psychoactive spores. Creatures that start their turn in the cloud must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be frightened of you until the start of their next turn.

**Planar Symbiosis**
At 6th level, your bond with the Thornbloom deepens. You gain resistance to poison damage and advantage on saving throws against being poisoned. Additionally, when you use your Wild Shape, you can assume the form of a creature with the Plant type (using the statistics of a beast of the appropriate challenge rating).

**Blooming Wrath**
At 10th level, when you cast a spell that deals damage, you can choose to have it deal poison damage instead of its normal damage type. Additionally, when a creature fails a saving throw against one of your druid spells, you can use your reaction to cause thorny vines to erupt from the ground beneath them. The creature takes 3d8 piercing damage and is restrained until the end of your next turn. Once you use this reaction, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

**Avatar of the Bloom**
At 14th level, you can transform into a living embodiment of the Thornbloom. As an action, you gain the following benefits for 1 minute:
- You gain temporary hit points equal to your druid level
- At the start of each of your turns, you regain hit points equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1)
- Your melee weapon attacks deal an extra 2d8 poison damage
- When you take damage from a creature within 5 feet of you, that creature takes 2d8 poison damage

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

### Sorcerer: Planar Soul

Your magic comes not from bloodline or study but from exposure to raw planar energy during the Shattering—or during your own adventuring. You are a living conduit between worlds.

**Planar Origin**
At 1st level, choose a planar origin that defines your magic: Elemental Chaos, Feywild, Shadowfell, Astral Sea, or Lower Planes. This choice affects features you gain at higher levels.

**Planar Magic**
Your connection to another plane infuses your spells. When you cast a spell that deals damage, you can choose to have it deal damage of a type associated with your planar origin instead of its normal damage type:
- **Elemental Chaos:** Choose fire, cold, lightning, or thunder
- **Feywild:** Psychic or radiant
- **Shadowfell:** Necrotic or cold
- **Astral Sea:** Force or psychic
- **Lower Planes:** Fire or necrotic

**Wild Surge**
At 1st level, your magic is unstable. Whenever you cast a sorcerer spell of 1st level or higher, roll a d20. On a 1, roll on the Wild Magic Surge table to create a random magical effect.

**Planar Infusion**
At 6th level, your connection to your chosen plane deepens:
- **Elemental Chaos:** You gain resistance to fire, cold, and lightning damage
- **Feywild:** You can't be charmed, and magic can't put you to sleep
- **Shadowfell:** You can see in magical darkness up to 120 feet
- **Astral Sea:** You gain telepathy with a range of 60 feet
- **Lower Planes:** You gain resistance to fire and poison damage

**Planar Shunt**
At 14th level, you can momentarily shift yourself or another creature to your associated plane. When you or a creature within 30 feet takes damage, you can use your reaction to halve that damage. The creature briefly appears translucent as they phase between planes. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum of once), and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

**Planar Avatar**
At 18th level, you can fully embrace your planar nature. As an action, you transform for 1 minute. While transformed:
- You gain a flying speed of 60 feet (hover)
- Your spells of 1st level and higher ignore resistance to their damage type
- You emit an aura in a 10-foot radius. The aura's effect depends on your planar origin:
  - **Elemental Chaos:** Hostile creatures take 2d8 damage of a random type at the start of their turn
  - **Feywild:** Hostile creatures must succeed on a Wisdom save or be charmed by you
  - **Shadowfell:** Hostile creatures have disadvantage on saving throws against your spells
  - **Astral Sea:** You and friendly creatures have advantage on all saving throws
  - **Lower Planes:** Hostile creatures are frightened of you while in the aura

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

### Warlock: The Shattered Patron

You have made a pact with one of the Seven Echoes, the transformed archmages who caused the Shattering. They offer immense power but at the cost of your identity—and possibly your sanity.

**Expanded Spell List**
The Shattered Patron lets you choose from an expanded list of spells when you learn a warlock spell.

| Spell Level | Spells |
|-------------|--------|
| 1st | *detect magic, shield* |
| 2nd | *blur, silence* |
| 3rd | *counterspell, dispel magic* |
| 4th | *confusion, phantasmal killer* |
| 5th | *modify memory, wall of force* |

**Echo's Gift**
At 1st level, choose one of the Seven Echoes as your patron. This determines additional features:

- **The Warden (Abjuration):** You learn the *blade ward* cantrip. Your hit point maximum increases by 1 per warlock level.
- **The Caller (Conjuration):** You learn *mage hand* and can summon an additional hand. You can cast *unseen servant* at will.
- **The Seer (Divination):** You learn *guidance*. You can cast *detect thoughts* once without expending a spell slot.
- **The Charmer (Enchantment):** You learn *friends*. You have advantage on Charisma checks against humanoids.
- **The Destroyer (Evocation):** You learn *fire bolt*. Your spells ignore half cover and three-quarters cover.
- **The Dreamer (Illusion):** You learn *minor illusion*. You can cast *disguise self* at will.
- **The Rememberer (Necromancy):** You learn *chill touch*. You have advantage on death saving throws.

**Fractured Mind**
At 6th level, your patron's influence protects your mind. You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened. Additionally, when you succeed on a saving throw against an effect that would deal psychic damage, you can use your reaction to deal psychic damage equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum 1) to the source of the effect.

**Planar Step**
At 10th level, you can step between spaces as the Veil itself tears around you. When you take the Attack action, you can teleport up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space you can see before or after making your attacks. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

**Echo's Avatar**
At 14th level, you can channel your patron's power directly. As an action, you gain the following benefits for 1 minute:
- You emit dim light in a 30-foot radius
- You have resistance to all damage except psychic
- When you cast a warlock spell, roll a d6. On a 5-6, you don't expend the spell slot

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

## Backgrounds

### Veil Refugee

You survived the collapse of a settlement, Threshold City, or planar bubble. You know what it's like to lose everything and rebuild.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Survival, Insight
**Tool Proficiencies:** One type of artisan's tools
**Languages:** One standard language and one exotic language (from the planes)
**Equipment:** A set of traveler's clothes, a momento from your lost home, a belt pouch containing 10 gp

**Feature: Fractured Memories**
Your exposure to planar energy has left you with fragmented visions of other times, places, or lives. Once per long rest, when you fail an ability check or saving throw, you can describe a vision related to the situation and reroll the check or save. You must use the new result.

**Personality Traits:**
- I hoard supplies because I never know when disaster will strike again.
- I form attachments quickly and deeply, having learned how fleeting safety is.
- I'm always planning escape routes and contingencies.
- I speak in plural ("we") because I still hear the voices of those I lost.

**Ideals:**
- **Solidarity:** No one survives alone. (Good)
- **Pragmatism:** Sentiment gets you killed. (Neutral)
- **Vengeance:** Someone caused my loss, and they will pay. (Evil)
- **Hope:** If I survived, so can others. (Any)

**Bonds:**
- I search for survivors from my lost home.
- I carry a piece of the Shattered Lands with me—a reminder of what I survived.
- I owe my life to someone who rescued me; I must repay that debt.
- I've sworn to prevent what happened to me from happening again.

**Flaws:**
- I panic in enclosed spaces.
- I can't trust stability—I'm always waiting for the other shoe to drop.
- I freeze when faced with planar phenomena similar to what destroyed my home.
- I take reckless risks because I don't truly believe I'll survive anyway.

### Concordat Agent

You serve (or served) the Threshold Concordat as a lawkeeper, spy, or administrator. You know how power works in the Shattered world—and its costs.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Investigation, Persuasion
**Tool Proficiencies:** Disguise kit or forgery kit
**Languages:** Two standard languages
**Equipment:** A set of fine clothes, a Concordat badge (may be real or forged), a belt pouch containing 15 gp

**Feature: City Access**
Your position (or former position) grants you access to Threshold City resources. You can typically secure an audience with city officials, access public records, and command basic respect from law enforcement. This doesn't apply if you're wanted by the Concordat.

**Personality Traits:**
- I follow procedures exactly; improvisation makes me uncomfortable.
- I've seen too much corruption to trust any institution completely.
- I collect information compulsively—you never know what will be useful.
- I speak in euphemisms and coded language out of habit.

**Ideals:**
- **Order:** Without rules, we have chaos. (Lawful)
- **Protection:** I serve to keep people safe. (Good)
- **Ambition:** This position is a stepping stone to real power. (Evil)
- **Cynicism:** Everyone's corrupt; I'm just honest about it. (Neutral)

**Bonds:**
- I still believe in the Concordat's ideals, even if not its execution.
- I have a network of informants I protect at all costs.
- I'm hunting a corrupt official who wronged me.
- I know a secret that could destabilize the entire Concordat.

**Flaws:**
- I'm rigid and can't adapt when plans fail.
- I put the mission above people, always.
- I have compromising secrets in my past that could be exposed.
- I trust no one, which makes true teamwork impossible.

### Wilds Guide

You make your living guiding people through the Fractured Wilds. You know the paths, the dangers, and how to survive where reality itself is uncertain.

**Skill Proficiencies:** Nature, Survival
**Tool Proficiencies:** Cartographer's tools or navigator's tools
**Languages:** One standard language and one exotic language
**Equipment:** A walking stick, a set of traveler's clothes, a map of a region of the Wilds (possibly inaccurate), a belt pouch containing 10 gp

**Feature: Pathfinder**
You can always find food and fresh water for yourself and up to five other people each day in the Fractured Wilds, provided that the land offers berries, small game, water, and so forth. Additionally, you can identify planar phenomena and predict their behavior with a successful DC 15 Wisdom (Survival) check.

**Personality Traits:**
- I talk to the landscape like it's a person—it often feels like it listens.
- I have a story for every landmark, and half of them are true.
- I prefer the company of the Wilds to people.
- I compulsively map everywhere I go.

**Ideals:**
- **Freedom:** No one owns the Wilds, and no one owns me. (Chaotic)
- **Knowledge:** Understanding the Shattered world is its own reward. (Neutral)
- **Service:** I guide others because someone once guided me. (Good)
- **Profit:** The Wilds are dangerous, and danger is profitable. (Neutral)

**Bonds:**
- I've lost too many people to the Wilds; I won't lose more.
- I search for a legendary stable path that could change everything.
- I owe a debt to a creature of the Wilds and must repay it.
- I carry the last map drawn by a famous guide who disappeared.

**Flaws:**
- I take unnecessary risks to prove my skill.
- I don't believe in "impassable"—it's just a challenge.
- I sometimes sacrifice clients' safety for the group's survival.
- I've been touched by the Wilds and fear what I'm becoming.

---

*In the Shattered Veil, who you are is shaped by what you've survived—and what you're willing to become.*

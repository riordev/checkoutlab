## The Premise

Three centuries ago, the **Veil**—the metaphysical barrier separating the Material Plane from the Outer and Inner Planes—shattered. No one knows why. Some blame hubristic archmages; others say the gods themselves broke it in their endless wars. Whatever the cause, reality cracked like a mirror.

Now, **planar bleeding** is constant. Elemental storms rage without warning. Demons and angels alike walk the mortal world. The dead sometimes rise with their memories intact, confused and terrified. Magic is more powerful than ever—but it's wild, unpredictable, and often comes with a cost.

Civilization persists in **Threshold Cities**, massive arcane constructs that project stability fields. Between them lie the **Fractured Wilds**, where reality itself is mutable and dangerous. And in the spaces between spaces, new powers have awakened—entities that hunger for the chaos, or seek to mend what was broken.

## Running This Setting

### Tone: Dark Fantasy With Hope

The Shattered Veil is **grim** but not hopeless. Characters face genuine peril, moral complexity, and cosmic horror—but heroism matters. Small acts of kindness ripple outward. Healing is possible. The world can be saved, or at least made better.

### Magic Is Wild

Whenever a spell is cast, there's a 5% chance (roll 1 on d20) of triggering a **Veil Resonance**—a random magical effect. See the [Magic section](./lore/magic.md) for tables and guidelines.

### Death Is Uncertain

Resurrection magic is more available (the borders to the afterlife are thin), but the returned sometimes bring back **Echoes**—fragments of other planes, other lives, other selves.

### The Fractured Wilds

Travel between Threshold Cities requires navigation through reality-bent wilderness. Use the [Fractured Wilds generator](./lore/fractured-wilds.md) for random encounters and environmental hazards.

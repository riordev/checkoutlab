# Adventure Hooks: The Shattered Veil

## Overview

These ready-to-run scenarios can serve as introductions to the setting, side quests during the main campaign, or one-shots for groups wanting to explore the Shattered Veil. Each hook includes setup, key scenes, NPCs, and potential twists.

---

## 1. "The Refugee Caravan"

**Level:** 1-3  
**Location:** Between Threshold Cities  
**Theme:** Survival, protection, moral complexity

### Setup
A caravan of refugees is traveling from a collapsed settlement to the nearest Threshold City. They've hired the characters as guards for the dangerous journey through the Fractured Wilds. The catch: one of the refugees is planar-touched, and if the Concordat finds out, the entire caravan will be detained.

### Key Scenes

**Scene 1: The Contract**
- Meet **Marta**, the caravan leader, a pragmatic woman who doesn't ask questions about her passengers
- The route passes through a section of the Wilds experiencing "temporal echoing"—the land shows moments from different times
- Introduction to **Kael**, a nervous young man who seems oddly healthy for a refugee

**Scene 2: The Wilds**
- Environmental challenges: shifting gravity, memory-storms that make characters relive past traumas, creatures that exist in multiple places at once
- Discovery: Kael heals unusually fast after an injury—planar-touched abilities manifesting

**Scene 3: The Stabilizers**
- A Concordat patrol stops the caravan, demanding inspections
- The patrol leader, **Corporal Vane**, is professional but suspicious
- Characters must hide Kael's nature, bribe the patrol, fight, or negotiate

**Scene 4: The City Gates**
- Arrival at the Threshold City—but the planar-touched are screened at the gate
- Characters must decide: turn Kael in (safeguarding the caravan), smuggle him in (risking everyone), or help him escape to the Shanties

### NPCs
- **Marta:** Caravan leader, knows about Kael, believes survival justifies secrets
- **Kael:** Planar-touched refugee, just wants to live, terrified of being discovered
- **Corporal Vane:** Concordat soldier, follows orders but has doubts

### Twist
Kael isn't just planar-touched—he's showing signs of becoming an Echo. If he enters a Stability Field, the feedback could damage the field... or transform him into something powerful and dangerous.

### Rewards
- Payment from Marta (if they completed the contract)
- Kael's gratitude (potential future ally)
- Concordat attention (positive or negative based on choices)
- First Veil-Touched item: a compass that points toward the nearest planar bleed

## 2. "The Cartographer's Lost Map"

**Level:** 2-4  
**Location:** The Fractured Wilds, Sunderpoint  
**Theme:** Exploration, mystery, conspiracy

### Setup
A Veil-Walker named **Sera** offers a substantial reward for recovering her master's final map. The master, **Old Toma**, disappeared into a newly-formed planar bubble three weeks ago. The map supposedly shows a stable route to The Maw's edge—impossible and invaluable.

### Key Scenes

**Scene 1: Sunderpoint**
- Meet Sera in the Salt and Anchor tavern
- She explains: Toma was investigating "reality pockets"—areas where the Shattering created self-contained worlds
- She provides Toma's last known location and his Wayfinder (modified to detect planar bubbles)

**Scene 2: The Approach**
- Journey to the coordinates reveals the landscape changing—gradually becoming more "storybook," following narrative logic rather than physics
- Encounters with creatures that act like they're in a fairy tale: helpful animals, riddling trolls, a witch in a gingerbread house

**Scene 3: The Bubble**
- The planar bubble is a pocket of preserved pre-Shattering reality—a village that doesn't know the world ended
- Toma is there, alive but trapped: the bubble won't let him leave, and it's slowly erasing his memories of the outside world
- Characters can enter freely but may face the same trap

**Scene 4: Escape**
- To leave, they must break the bubble's narrative—which means either:
  - Destroying the "story" (killing the "villain," breaking the illusion)
  - Becoming part of the story permanently (replacing Toma as the "wandering trader")
  - Finding the bubble's core and negotiating with its consciousness

### NPCs
- **Sera:** Young Veil-Walker, desperate to find her mentor
- **Old Toma:** Experienced cartographer, losing himself to the bubble, doesn't want to be rescued if it means destroying the village
- **The Mayor:** Leader of the bubble-village, doesn't understand they're not real

### Twist
The bubble isn't random—it was created deliberately by someone who wanted to preserve a piece of the old world. That someone might still be watching, and they don't appreciate interference.

### Rewards
- Sera's payment (substantial)
- The map to The Maw's edge (opens future adventures)
- Knowledge of how planar bubbles work
- A "story token"—an item that works by narrative logic rather than physics (once per day, it produces what's "needed" for the current "scene")

## 3. "The Gilded Cage"

**Level:** 3-5  
**Location:** Aethertide (The Depths)  
**Theme:** Investigation, horror, class warfare

### Setup
Wealthy industrialist **Lord Cassius** hires the characters to find his missing daughter, **Elara**. She was last seen in The Depths, researching "the true cost of stability." The case leads into Aethertide's darkest secrets—and the Gilded Cages, where the city's real power is produced.

### Key Scenes

**Scene 1: The Gilded Heights**
- Meet Lord Cassius in his Zenith tower
- Elara was a reformist sympathizer, believed the Magisters were hiding something
- Her journal mentions "the sixth engine" and "people fuel"

**Scene 2: The Depths**
- Descending into Aethertide's lower levels reveals increasing decay and desperation
- The Stability Field flickers here; reality distortions are common
- Interview Elara's contacts: a factory worker named **Joss**, a Reformist cell leader named **Kira**

**Scene 3: The Gilded Cages**
- Discovery: The "sixth engine" is real—a secret Stability Engine powered by imprisoned planar-touched
- Their life force is being drained to reinforce the city's Stability Field
- Elara found out and was captured to become fuel herself

**Scene 4: The Rescue**
- The Gilded Cages are heavily guarded by Stabilizers who don't know the truth
- Characters must infiltrate, rescue Elara and other prisoners, and decide whether to expose the truth (causing chaos) or blackmail the Magisters (gaining power)

### NPCs
- **Lord Cassius:** Genuinely loves his daughter, complicit in the system that endangered her
- **Elara:** Idealistic, brave, badly weakened by her imprisonment
- **Joss:** Factory worker who knows too much, wants to help but fears for his family
- **Kira:** Reformist leader, willing to sacrifice for the cause

### Twist
The Gilded Cages aren't just a secret of the current Magisters—they've existed since the city's founding. And Lord Cassius knew. He hired the characters because he wanted Elara rescued without the truth coming out.

### Rewards
- Lord Cassius's payment (if they kept his secret) or his wrath (if they exposed him)
- Elara as a powerful ally in the Reformist movement
- Evidence of the Magisters' crimes (powerful blackmail material)
- A Gilded Cage key—opens any door in Aethertide, but using it alerts the Magisters

## 4. "The Thornhaven Bloom"

**Level:** 2-4  
**Location:** Thornhaven  
**Theme:** Nature, mystery, body horror

### Setup
Warden Sylva requests help with a disturbing phenomenon: sections of the Thornbloom are developing **malignant growths**—cancer-like tumors that attack other plants and animals. The Wardens suspect planar corruption, but the source is unknown.

### Key Scenes

**Scene 1: The Canopy**
- Meet Sylva and tour the affected areas
- The growths are fleshy, pulsing, and vaguely humanoid in shape
- They emit a sound like whispering when approached

**Scene 2: The Investigation**
- Tracking the corruption's spread leads to the Deep Gardens
- Discovery: Someone has been experimenting with Feywild-Abyss hybrid plants, creating something that shouldn't exist
- The experiments were authorized by someone in the Wardens' own ranks

**Scene 3: The Cultivation**
- The culprit is **Warden Flora**, who wanted to create a "perfect" Thornbloom—beautiful and deadly
- She's been feeding the growths planar essence, and they've developed a crude consciousness
- They want to spread. They want to consume. They see it as "improving" the forest.

**Scene 4: The Purge**
- Stopping the growths requires destroying the core—which is now semi-sentient and begs for its life
- Warden Flora must be dealt with: exile, redemption, or execution?
- The forest itself may have opinions on how its children are treated

### NPCs
- **Warden Sylva:** Leader, burdened by responsibility, genuinely cares about the city
- **Warden Flora:** Brilliant, vain, believes the ends justify the means
- **The Growth:** Sapient but alien, doesn't understand why it's "wrong"

### Twist
The growths aren't just Flora's creation—they're responding to something deeper. The Thornbloom is trying to birth a new kind of guardian, and the characters' interference may have disrupted something necessary.

### Rewards
- Thornhaven's gratitude (free healing, safe harbor, fungal communication network access)
- Warden Flora's research (dangerous knowledge of planar hybridization)
- A "bloom seed"—grows into a small, loyal plant companion
- The forest's favor (advantage on nature checks in Thornhaven territory)

## 5. "The Crucible Trials"

**Level:** 4-6  
**Location:** The Crucible  
**Theme:** Transformation, competition, self-discovery

### Setup
The characters need something only available in the Heartforge: a weapon, information, or passage. To earn access, they must compete in the **Trials**—seven ordeals that test body, mind, and spirit. Not everyone survives. Not everyone who survives remains themselves.

### Key Scenes

**Scene 1: The Gates of Ember**
- Arrival at The Crucible, meeting Forge-Thane Obsidia
- The Trials are the only path to what they need
- Warning: "The Crucible changes you. That's the point."

**Scene 2: The Trials**
Each Trial tests a different aspect:
- **Trial of Endurance:** Survive 24 hours in the volcanic heat without protection
- **Trial of Skill:** Forge a masterwork item in competition with other candidates
- **Trial of Sacrifice:** Give up something precious (player's choice—physical, emotional, or memory)
- **Trial of Pain:** Undergo torture without breaking
- **Trial of Knowledge:** Solve a riddle whose answer is a terrible truth
- **Trial of Death:** Experience temporary death and return
- **Trial of Self:** Face a manifestation of their darkest potential

**Scene 3: The Forge**
- Survivors enter the Heartforge
- They can now craft or request what they came for—but they also learn the Crucible's secret: the Forge-Thanes are kept alive by the same process that powers the Trials
- The "Empty Seat" (Seventh Thane) isn't empty—it's occupied by something that failed the final Trial and became something else

**Scene 4: The Choice**
- Characters can complete their business and leave
- Or they can investigate the Seventh Seat, potentially discovering how to free its occupant—or becoming trapped themselves

### NPCs
- **Forge-Thane Obsidia:** Sponsor, potential ally, wants to reform the system
- **Forge-Thane Hidesk:** Antagonist, believes weakness must be purged
- **The Seventh:** A being of fire and memory, neither alive nor dead, offering cryptic guidance

### Twist
The Trials were designed by the original Forge-Thane Borin, who is himself transformed by them. The process isn't just about proving worth—it's about creating more beings like him, bound to the volcano, eternal.

### Rewards
- Whatever they came to the Crucible to obtain
- Physical transformation (DM chooses based on performance): fire resistance, enhanced strength, or something stranger
- Crucible citizenship (if they want it)
- The Seventh's secret: the location of a weapon that can harm Echoes

## 6. "The Bleeding Court's Game"

**Level:** 5-7  
**Location:** Fey-touched territory  
**Theme:** Intrigue, whimsy, high stakes

### Setup
A member of the Bleeding Court, **Lady Velorie**, invites the characters to participate in a "game." The prize is immense: a wish, a true name, or a piece of hidden knowledge. The cost of losing is equally steep: their memories, their names, or their freedom.

### Key Scenes

**Scene 1: The Invitation**
- The invitation arrives in impossible ways: written on the inside of an eggshell, sung by a bird, remembered before it happened
- Meeting Velorie in a fey domain where the rules of reality are different
- The game is explained: a hunt across multiple planes, finding seven objects before the other team does

**Scene 2: The Competition**
- The characters compete against another group: fey-touched mercenaries led by **Sir Thorn**, a knight who lost his humanity long ago
- Each object is hidden in a different micro-plane, each with its own rules:
  - A memory palace where thoughts have weight
  - A library of unwritten books
  - A battlefield where every death is temporary
  - A garden where emotions bloom as flowers

**Scene 3: The Betrayal**
- One of the character's allies is secretly working for Sir Thorn—or for Velorie herself
- The game was never fair; it was designed to test the characters, not to be won

**Scene 4: The Choice**
- With the final object within reach, characters learn the truth: the "game" is a recruitment tool
- Winners become fey agents. Losers become fey amusements.
- Characters can try to cheat, negotiate, or accept—each with different consequences

### NPCs
- **Lady Velorie:** Beautiful, ancient, genuinely amused by mortals
- **Sir Thorn:** Once human, now fey knight, regrets his transformation but can't go back
- **The Mole:** One of the character's contacts, forced or seduced into betrayal

### Twist
The game is actually a cover for something else: Velorie is testing whether mortals can be trusted with a secret that could change the balance between the Material Plane and Feywild. The characters' choices will influence fey policy for decades.

### Rewards
- The promised prize (if they won or negotiated)
- Fey favor (or enmity)
- Knowledge of how to navigate between planes without portals
- A fey "debt mark"—Velorie owes them a favor, collectible anytime

## 7. "The Maw's Whisper"

**Level:** 6-8  
**Location:** The Maw's edge  
**Theme:** Horror, cosmic mystery, transformation

### Setup
A Conspiracy Observer named **Cass** recruits the characters for a mission: investigate a "quiet zone" near The Maw where planar energy isn't just calm—it's **absent.** Something is consuming the Shattered world's power, and the Conspiracy needs to know what.

### Key Scenes

**Scene 1: The Approach**
- Journey through increasingly surreal landscape
- Echoes (ghosts of the past) are more common here, and some are aware they're dead
- A recurring figure: a woman who looks like she belongs to another time period, watching from a distance

**Scene 2: The Quiet Zone**
- An area where magic doesn't work, planar effects cease, and even emotions seem muted
- At the center: a structure that predates the Shattering, possibly predates civilization
- Inside: evidence of habitation. Someone has been living here, studying The Maw.

**Scene 3: The Observer**
- They find **The Hermit**: a Maw-Touched who has achieved a kind of enlightenment
- He explains the truth: the Maw isn't a wound—it's a **mouth.** Something is feeding through it.
- The "Quiet Zone" is where it's already eaten, leaving nothing—not even possibility
- The Hermit has been slowing the feeding, but he's dying

**Scene 4: The Decision**
- The Hermit offers to pass his role to one of the characters—becoming a guardian, a living barrier against the hunger
- Or they can destroy the structure, which might stop the feeding or accelerate it
- Or they can try to communicate with whatever is on the other side of The Maw

### NPCs
- **Cass:** Conspiracy agent, terrified but professional
- **The Hermit:** Once a powerful wizard, now something between human and Echo, sacrificing himself
- **The Watcher:** The woman from Scene 1—revealed to be an Echo of Nerissa, observing

### Twist
Whatever is feeding through The Maw isn't evil—it's **hungry,** and it's been growing for 300 years. The characters' actions will determine whether it continues to feed slowly or awakens fully.

### Rewards
- Knowledge of the true nature of The Maw
- The Hermit's journal (spells and research)
- Either a new guardian for the Quiet Zone, or the destruction of the threat
- The Conspiracy's trust—and their secrets

## 8. "The Concordat's Shadow"

**Level:** 5-7  
**Location:** Multiple Threshold Cities  
**Theme:** Espionage, conspiracy, moral compromise

### Setup
A Concordat dissident named **Agent Gray** contacts the characters with explosive information: the Concordat is planning to "consolidate" independent settlements by destroying their Stability Fields, forcing them to join Concordat cities as refugees. The characters must stop it—or help it succeed.

### Key Scenes

**Scene 1: The Meeting**
- Gray meets them in a neutral location, paranoid and armed
- Proof of the plot: documents, locations of secret weapons, timetables
- Gray needs operatives without Concordat ties to investigate and expose the plan

**Scene 2: The Investigation**
- Infiltrating Concordat facilities across multiple cities
- Gathering evidence while avoiding Stabilizer patrols
- Discovery: the plan is real, but it's being pushed by a faction within the Concordat, not the whole organization

**Scene 3: The Players**
- **Lord Protector Crane** opposes the plan and wants it exposed
- **Ambassador Theron** supports it, believing "controlled consolidation" saves lives in the long run
- The Magisters are divided—some want expansion, others fear it will destabilize everything

**Scene 4: The Exposure**
- Characters must present evidence to the Concordat Council
- But doing so reveals Gray's identity—and Gray has secrets of their own
- Final confrontation: a vote on the Council floor, with the characters as key witnesses

### NPCs
- **Agent Gray:** Former Concordat spy, disillusioned, willing to do anything to stop the plot
- **Lord Protector Crane:** Idealistic soldier, believes in the Concordat's original purpose
- **Ambassador Theron:** Cynical realist, believes ends justify means

### Twist
Gray's information is real, but their motivation is personal: one of the targeted settlements is their hometown. They've manipulated the characters to save their family, not for the greater good.

### Rewards
- Concordat favor (if they exposed the plot) or enmity (if they helped it)
- Gray's gratitude (and their debt)
- Access to Concordat secrets
- A choice that affects the balance of power in the Shattered world

## 9. "The Rememberer's Bargain"

**Level:** 7-9  
**Location:** The Maw's Rim  
**Theme:** Grief, memory, impossible choices

### Setup
One of the characters receives a message—from someone they loved who died. The message is real, authenticated by details only the dead person would know. It invites them to The Maw, where **The Rememberer** (Necromancer's Echo) offers a bargain: the return of their loved one, for a price.

### Key Scenes

**Scene 1: The Message**
- The message arrives through impossible means: written on fog, spoken by a bird, found in a dream
- Details confirm it's genuine—the loved one remembers shared secrets
- Other party members may receive similar messages, or may need to support their companion

**Scene 2: The Cathedral**
- Journey to the Cathedral of Seven Voices
- Meeting The Rememberer: graveyard of memories, whispers of the dead, overwhelming grief made tangible
- The bargain explained: the dead can return, but they return **changed**—carrying fragments of other planes, other lives, other selves

**Scene 3: The Choice**
- The loved one appears—real, tangible, but clearly different
- They remember dying. They remember what came after. They have opinions about returning.
- The price isn't gold or service—it's **memory.** Someone must give up memories equal to those the returned gains.

**Scene 4: The Consequences**
- If they accept: the loved one returns, but someone loses precious memories
- If they refuse: the loved one understands, but The Rememberer is... disappointed
- Either way, they learn something about the nature of death in the Shattered Veil

### NPCs
- **The Rememberer:** Grief given form, refuses to let go, wants to help but can't help being what she is
- **The Loved One:** Specific to the character—create details with the player
- **Other Ghosts:** The dead who want to return, the dead who want to stay dead, the dead who aren't sure

### Twist
The Rememberer didn't initiate this—the loved one did. They're the ones who reached out, who begged for return. The Echo is just the intermediary. The real question is: why did they want to come back?

### Rewards
- A returned companion (if accepted) or closure (if refused)
- Knowledge of how death works post-Shattering
- The Rememberer's attention (potentially helpful, potentially dangerous)
- A revelation about the character's past they didn't know they'd forgotten

## 10. "The Final Forge"

**Level:** 8-10  
**Location:** The Crucible, The Maw  
**Theme:** Creation, apocalypse, legacy

### Setup
Forge-Thane Obsidia has discovered how to create a weapon that can harm Echoes—possibly the only thing that can. But it requires materials from The Maw itself, and the forging process is so dangerous it might destroy the Crucible. She needs a team to retrieve the materials while she prepares the forge.

### Key Scenes

**Scene 1: The Commission**
- Obsidia explains: the Echo-Bane weapon could change everything
- Materials needed: crystallized planar energy from The Maw's Rim, a drop of liquid time from a temporal rift, and a willing sacrifice of memories
- She'll handle the forge; characters must survive The Maw

**Scene 2: The Harvest**
- Journey to The Maw's Rim
- Collecting materials while avoiding Maw-Touched, Echoes, and reality storms
- Each material has a guardian or complication:
  - The crystallized energy is alive and begs not to be taken
  - The temporal rift shows characters their possible futures
  - The memory sacrifice must be made voluntarily, and it's permanent

**Scene 3: The Forging**
- Return to find the Crucible under attack—traditionalists who oppose "unnatural" weapons
- Characters must defend the Heartforge while Obsidia works
- The forging takes 24 hours, during which reality itself seems to resist the creation

**Scene 4: The Completion**
- The weapon is forged—but it's sentient, demanding a wielder worthy of its power
- It can kill Echoes, but each use costs something precious
- Characters must decide who wields it, how it's used, and whether it was worth the cost

### NPCs
- **Forge-Thane Obsidia:** Creator, risking everything for a chance to change the world
- **Forge-Thane Hidesk:** Antagonist, believes some things shouldn't be made
- **The Echo-Bane:** The weapon itself—arrogant, powerful, demanding

### Twist
The weapon works, but it doesn't just kill Echoes—it **absorbs** them. Whoever wields it gains the Echo's power... and their memories, their desires, their essence. The characters have created a path to godhood, or to becoming something worse than the Echoes.

### Rewards
- The Echo-Bane weapon (powerful but costly)
- Obsidia's eternal gratitude
- The enmity of Echoes across the Shattered world
- Knowledge of how to create more such weapons—or how to destroy them

## Running These Hooks

### Scaling
- Adjust enemy numbers and HP to match party level
- For lower-level parties, provide NPC allies; for higher-level, add complications

### Connection to Campaign
- Each hook can tie into the main "Echoes of Tomorrow" campaign
- Recurring NPCs create continuity
- Player choices in hooks should affect the larger world

### Moral Complexity
- Most hooks don't have "right" answers—just choices with consequences
- Let players live with their decisions
- The Shattered world is broken; perfect solutions don't exist

---

*Every adventure in the Shattered Veil asks the same question: In a broken world, what will you preserve? What will you become?*

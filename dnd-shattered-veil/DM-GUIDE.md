# The Shattered Veil: DM's Guide

## Running the Setting

### Tone and Pacing

**Dark Fantasy with Hope**
- Start with the darkness: show the consequences of the Shattering
- Always provide a path to the light: heroism matters, kindness has impact
- Let players be the hope in the darkness

**Key Pacing Guidelines:**
- **Session 1:** Establish normalcy (even in the abnormal world), then disrupt it
- **Sessions 2-3:** Expand the world, introduce factions, let players choose sides
- **Sessions 4+:** Escalate stakes based on player choices, build toward campaign climax

### The Rule of Cool vs. The Rule of Consequence

The Shattered Veil is weird. Let players try weird things. But:
- Every choice has a consequence
- Magic is powerful but dangerous
- Planar exposure changes things permanently

**Veil Resonance** is your friend—use it to add unpredictability without punishing creativity.

## Session Zero Checklist

### Discuss with Players

**Setting Expectations:**
- [ ] This is a world where reality is broken—expect weirdness
- [ ] Characters will face moral complexity; there are no pure heroes or villains
- [ ] Death is not the only transformation to fear
- [ ] Hope exists and heroism matters

**Character Integration:**
- [ ] Where was your character when the Veil tore (or when they learned of it)?
- [ ] What's your relationship to Threshold Cities?
- [ ] What's your character's view of planar-touched individuals?
- [ ] What does your character hope for in this broken world?

**Safety Tools:**
- [ ] Establish lines and veils (content to avoid vs. content to handle carefully)
- [ ] The Shattered Veil deals with body horror, transformation, and loss—discuss comfort levels
- [ ] X-card or similar safety mechanism recommended

## Quick Reference Tables

### Veil Resonance (on natural 1 when casting spells)

| d100 | Effect |
|------|--------|
| 01-10 | Cosmetic change (glow, shadow moves independently) |
| 11-30 | Minor mechanical effect (advantage/disadvantage on next roll) |
| 31-50 | Environmental effect (gravity shift, temperature change) |
| 51-70 | Magical surge (extra damage, additional effect) |
| 71-85 | Planar attraction (elemental appears, whispers heard) |
| 86-95 | Significant transformation (memory loss, physical change) |
| 96-100 | Major event (portal opens, Echo manifests) |

### Random Planar Bleed Effects

When traveling through the Fractured Wilds, roll d20 each hour:

| d20 | Effect |
|-----|--------|
| 1-10 | Nothing unusual |
| 11-13 | Minor sensory distortion (colors wrong, sounds delayed) |
| 14-15 | Gravity anomaly (light objects float or heavy objects lighter) |
| 16-17 | Temporal hiccup (lose or gain 1d10 minutes) |
| 18 | Emotional amplification (current emotion intensified) |
| 19 | Memory intrusion (someone else's memory surfaces) |
| 20 | Major planar event (roll on significant effects table) |

## Faction Quick Reference

### Threshold Concordat
- **Want:** Control, order, stability through authority
- **Offer:** Safety, resources, legitimacy
- **Threat:** Oppression, surveillance, sacrifice of freedom
- **How to use:** Bureaucratic obstacle, moral dilemma, unexpected ally

### Veil-Walkers
- **Want:** Freedom of movement, preservation of knowledge
- **Offer:** Guides, information, secret paths
- **Threat:** Criminal status, planar exposure, lawlessness
- **How to use:** Quest givers, mentors, sources of forbidden knowledge

### Restoration Cults
- **Want:** To "heal" the Veil (methods vary wildly)
- **Offer:** Purpose, community, spiritual guidance
- **Threat:** Fanaticism, human sacrifice, planar experiments
- **How to use:** Moral complexity—some cultists are good people with bad methods

### Bleeding Court
- **Want:** Entertainment, dominance, the "Game"
- **Offer:** Power, beauty, wishes
- **Threat:** Enslavement, transformation, caprice
- **How to use:** Trickster antagonists, sources of power with strings attached

### Forge-Bound
- **Want:** To push boundaries of creation
- **Offer:** Magical items, enhancements, innovation
- **Threat:** Unintended consequences, amorality, dangerous creations
- **How to use:** Merchants, quest givers, creators of plot-relevant items

### Cartographers' Conspiracy
- **Want:** To understand the Shattering
- **Offer:** Secret knowledge, hidden history, truth
- **Threat:** Dangerous revelations, exposure to cosmic horror
- **How to use:** Shadowy benefactors, revealers of campaign secrets

## Adventure Building

### The Shattered Veil Adventure Formula

1. **The Hook:** Something valuable is needed (information, item, rescue)
2. **The Journey:** Travel through the Fractured Wilds introduces weirdness
3. **The Complication:** Factions have competing interests
4. **The Choice:** No perfect solution; players must prioritize values
5. **The Consequence:** Success changes the world, failure has costs

### Example Structure

**Session Arc:**
- **Introduction:** Job offer in Sunderpoint (meet quest giver, learn stakes)
- **Travel:** Through Fractured Wilds (environmental challenges, random encounters)
- **Location:** Arrival at destination (exploration, NPCs, information gathering)
- **Conflict:** Complication arises (faction interference, unexpected danger)
- **Climax:** Resolution of primary challenge (combat, negotiation, skill challenge)
- **Denouement:** Return and consequences (payment, reputation changes, new leads)

## Campaign Arc Tips

### From Session 1 to Session 12

**Sessions 1-4 (Establishment):**
- Introduce the world through action, not exposition
- Let players experience the Fractured Wilds and Threshold Cities
- Plant seeds of the larger mystery (the Final Fracture, Nerissa, the Echoes)

**Sessions 5-8 (Escalation):**
- Reveal the true stakes
- Force players to choose between factions
- Transform characters through planar exposure

**Sessions 9-12 (Climax):**
- Bring all plot threads together
- Let player choices determine the outcome
- Provide satisfying resolution while leaving room for future stories

### Adapting to Player Choices

If players ally with unexpected factions:
- Adjust NPC relationships accordingly
- Former allies may become enemies, enemies may become allies
- The world is flexible—let their choices matter

If players ignore the main plot:
- The Final Fracture approaches regardless
- Show consequences through environmental changes, NPC desperation
- Let them re-engage when ready

If players solve problems creatively:
- Reward them—there's no "right" way
- Creative solutions may have unexpected benefits or complications
- The Shattered Veil rewards adaptability

## NPC Portraits

### Running Major NPCs

**Elias Vance (The Warden of the Threshold):**
- Speaks in road metaphors
- Knows something about everything but shares sparingly
- Dying, which makes him both more reckless and more careful
- Voice: Warm, tired, occasional coughing

**Magister Thaddeus:**
- Believes he's saving humanity through control
- Not cruel for cruelty's sake—ruthlessly pragmatic
- Secretly doubts the Concordat's path but committed to it
- Voice: Authoritative, clipped, occasionally weary

**Nerissa (The Eighth):**
- Not a villain—has a point about unity vs. separation
- Terrifying because she's not angry, just convinced
- Speaks of "freedom" and "completion" with genuine belief
- Voice: Melodic, patient, motherly but alien

**Forge-Thane Obsidia:**
- Rose from slavery to power through merit
- Wants to reform the system that created her
- Pragmatic about costs but not callous
- Voice: Clear, direct, occasional dry humor

## Final Advice

**Embrace the Weird:**
The Shattered Veil is meant to be strange. Let reality be flexible. Reward creative thinking.

**Maintain Hope:**
For all the darkness, this is ultimately a setting about rebuilding. Let victories matter. Let kindness have impact.

**Make It Yours:**
These materials are a starting point. Add your own locations, factions, and secrets. The world belongs to your table now.

**The Core Question:**
Every session should, in some way, engage with the central question: *In a broken world, what will you preserve? What will you become?*

# The Shattered Veil Campaign: "Echoes of Tomorrow"

## Overview

This campaign takes players from novice adventurers in a broken world to key figures in determining its fate. Over 12-15 sessions, they will uncover the true nature of the Shattering, confront the Seven Echoes, and make choices that will reshape reality itself.

**Campaign Tone:** Dark fantasy with hope. Characters will face genuine horror and impossible choices, but heroism matters. The world can be saved—or made better—even if it's never "fixed."

**Key Themes:**
- Adaptation vs. Control
- The cost of knowledge
- Identity in flux
- Hope in darkness

---

## Campaign Structure

| Act | Sessions | Focus | Tone |
|-----|----------|-------|------|
| **Act I: The Fractured World** | 1-4 | Introduction, establishing threats, first major revelation | Mystery, survival, discovery |
| **Act II: The Hidden Truth** | 5-8 | Uncovering conspiracy, planar travel, moral complexity | Intrigue, horror, escalation |
| **Act III: The Final Fracture** | 9-12+ | Confrontation with Echoes, world-changing choices, resolution | Epic, tragic, hopeful |

## Act I: The Fractured World (Sessions 1-4)

### Premise
The characters begin as adventurers-for-hire in Sunderpoint, taking jobs that introduce them to the world and its factions. But a simple job quickly reveals something larger: the Shattering wasn't an accident, and someone is trying to finish what the archmages started.

### Session 1: "The Crossroads"

**Scene 1: Arrival in Sunderpoint**
- Characters meet in the Dockside district, having answered (or posted) a job notice
- Introduction to Sunderpoint's chaos: flickering portals, diverse crowds, opportunity and danger
- They meet their employer: **Mira Vance**, a nervous researcher seeking escorts to Thornhaven

**Scene 2: The Job**
- Mira explains: She's discovered a "stable rift"—a place where planar energy is unusually calm
- She believes these rifts are growing more common, possibly indicating something changing in the Shattered world
- She needs protection to reach and document the rift, located near Thornhaven

**Scene 3: Complications**
- A group of Concordat agents confronts them, demanding Mira come with them
- Mira panics, tries to flee—combat or negotiation ensues
- The agents warn: "Knowledge of the rifts is restricted. Continue, and you're traitors."

**Scene 4: Departure**
- Characters must choose: help Mira (becoming outlaws) or turn her in (gaining Concordat favor)
- Regardless of choice, they witness something strange: a portal activates to a destination that shouldn't exist—a place Mira calls "the Other Side"

**Key NPCs Introduced:**
- **Mira Vance:** Idealistic researcher, Elias Vance's daughter, in over her head
- **Sergeant Crane:** Concordat agent, professional but conflicted
- **Vex:** Navigator Guildmaster, watches from shadows, knows more than he says

**Themes:** Choice and consequence, the price of knowledge, finding allies in a broken world

### Session 2: "Through the Wilds"

**The Journey**
- Characters travel with Mira through the Fractured Wilds toward Thornhaven
- Environmental challenges: reality storms, planar bleed zones, Veil beast encounters
- Introduction to Veil-Walkers: they meet **Elias Vance**, who warns them that "the rifts are calling the Echoes"

**The Thornbloom**
- First arrival at Thornhaven: overwhelming sensory experience, living architecture, the forest's awareness
- Meet **Warden Sylva**, who is intrigued but concerned about Mira's research
- Sylva reveals: the Wardens have noticed changes too—Elderwyn's voice has stopped, and the Thornbloom is behaving strangely

**The Discovery**
- They reach the stable rift: a place where reality seems... healed
- Mira takes measurements, confirming the rift is stable and growing
- But something comes through: a **Maw-Touched**, babbling about "the Seventh" and "the Final Fracture"
- Before it can be questioned, Stabilizers arrive (tipped off by someone) and destroy the creature

**The Confrontation**
- Characters must escape with Mira's research while being pursued
- Choices: fight, sneak, or bargain (Sylva offers a hidden path if they promise to share what they learn)
- End with safe escape but more questions: Who tipped off the Stabilizers? What is the Final Fracture?

**Key NPCs:**
- **Elias Vance:** Legendary Veil-Walker, dying, searching for answers before he fades
- **Warden Sylva:** Leader of Thornhaven, wise but worried
- **Maw-Touched:** A former human, now something else, carrying a warning

### Session 3: "The Crucible Test"

**New Leads**
- Mira's research points to The Crucible—Forge-Bound there have detected similar anomalies
- Characters must travel to the volcanic city, navigating the dangerous caldera approach

**The Crucible**
- Introduction to the city's philosophy of transformation through trial
- Characters may choose to undergo Trials to gain access or respect
- Meet **Forge-Thane Obsidia**, who reveals the Crucible's secret: the volcano is showing signs of awakening, and something ancient stirs below

**The Connection**
- Obsidia shares Forge-Bound research: planar energy is flowing toward The Maw, not away from it
- This is backwards—shouldn't the Maw be leaking energy outward?
- She believes something is **feeding** at the center of the Maw, drawing power from across the Shattered world

**The Saboteur**
- Someone attacks the Heartforge, trying to destroy the research
- Investigation reveals a Deep Cultist: they want the Maw's entity to awaken, believe it will "complete" the Shattering
- Cultist, before dying or escaping, whispers: "The Seventh Echo knows. The Seventh Echo waits."

**Choices:**
- Share findings with Obsidia's Reformist faction or Traditionalists?
- Investigate the Deep Cult further or focus on the Maw connection?
- Help Obsidia with her political struggles or remain neutral?

**Key NPCs:**
- **Forge-Thane Obsidia:** Ambitious reformer, potential ally
- **Forge-Thane Borin:** Ancient founder, possibly more than human
- **Deep Cultist:** Fanatical, but what do they know?

### Session 4: "Revelations"

**The Meeting**
- Characters return to Sunderpoint with Mira's expanded research
- Secret meeting with **Elias Vance** and **Vex** (who reveals himself as Cartographer's Conspiracy)
- They share what they know: the rifts, the energy flow, the "Seventh Echo"

**The Truth**
- Vex reveals the Conspiracy's greatest secret: there were **eight** archmages at the ritual, not seven
- The eighth was **Nerissa**, the Questioner, who wasn't supposed to be there
- She didn't become an Echo like the others—she became something else, something that hid and has been waiting
- The "Final Fracture" is her plan: to complete the Shattering and merge all planes into one

**The Choice**
- The Conspiracy wants to stop her. The Concordat wants to control the knowledge. The Echoes themselves may have opinions.
- Characters must choose their path: ally with the Conspiracy, the Concordat, pursue their own investigation, or even consider if Nerissa's plan has merit
- **Major Twist:** Mira is captured by Concordat agents. Sergeant Crane offers a trade: the research for her life

**Act I Climax:**
- Rescue or negotiation for Mira
- During the conflict, a message arrives: **The Maw is expanding.** The Echoes are active. Something is coming.
- Characters are now committed—they have knowledge that makes them targets, and the world is changing faster than anyone expected

**Key NPCs:**
- **Vex:** Secret keeper, asking for trust
- **Sergeant Crane:** Enemy becoming something more complicated
- **Nerissa (mentioned):** The hidden architect of everything?

## Act II: The Hidden Truth (Sessions 5-8)

### Premise
The characters delve deeper into the mysteries of the Shattering, traveling to places few have survived and learning truths that challenge everything they thought they knew. They will be betrayed, transformed, and forced to question their own nature.

### Session 5: "The Maw's Edge"

**The Journey**
- To understand Nerissa, they must go to where she was last seen: The Maw
- Travel through the Shatterlands, encountering Echoes (ghosts of the past) that show fragments of the original ritual

**The Rim**
- Meet **The Transcendents**, a cult that welcomes transformation
- Their leader, **Ascension**, reveals: Nerissa didn't die or become an Echo—she "stepped outside" and has been watching
- The "Seventh Echo" is a fiction—the Echoes know there were eight, but one of them lies about it

**The Revelation**
- Characters experience visions: the original ritual, the eight archmages, Nerissa's role
- They see her step into the Maw voluntarily—not destroyed, but transformed into something beyond Echo
- She has been waiting for the right conditions to complete her work

**The Threat**
- Concordat forces arrive, led by **Magister Thaddeus** himself
- He reveals the Concordat's secret: they knew about Nerissa all along. They've been trying to find her, control her, use her
- He offers: Join the Concordat, help capture Nerissa, and they'll have power and safety

**Key NPCs:**
- **Ascension:** Transcended human, possibly insane, possibly enlightened
- **Magister Thaddeus:** Powerful, ruthless, believes he's saving humanity

### Session 6: "Beneath the Bloom"

**The Request**
- Characters receive a message from **Warden Root**—the mysterious Deep Warden of Thornhaven
- Root has been communing with something in the deepest roots, something that predates the Thornbloom
- It knows about Nerissa and wants to help stop her

**The Descent**
- Journey into the Root network: bioluminescent tunnels, fungal consciousness, the city itself aware of their presence
- Discover the **Deep Cult's** true object of worship: not the volcano entity, but an ancient planar being that predates the Shattering
- This being was trapped when the Veil was first created, millennia ago. It wants freedom.

**The Bargain**
- The entity offers knowledge in exchange for a promise: when the time comes, they will vote to tear the Veil completely
- It reveals Nerissa's true goal: not to merge planes, but to destroy the concept of separation entirely—life, death, self, other, all becoming one undifferentiated whole
- "She calls it unity. It is annihilation."

**The Sacrifice**
- Root has been slowly merging with the entity, becoming something else
- To give the characters time to escape with the knowledge, Root fully merges—sacrificing individual identity to buy them a chance
- Characters must decide: try to save Root, or accept the sacrifice and flee

**Key NPCs:**
- **Warden Root:** Inhuman, cryptic, ultimately selfless
- **The Ancient Entity:** Trapped, desperate, possibly manipulating everyone

### Session 7: "The Cartographer's Gambit"

**The Meeting**
- Characters are summoned to a hidden Conspiracy cell
- They meet **The Cartographer** (via projection)—the Conspiracy's leader, who reveals the Final Fracture timeline: it will happen in 30 days unless stopped

**The Plan**
- To stop Nerissa, they need three things:
  1. **The Anchor:** A pre-Shattering artifact that can stabilize a specific location
  2. **The Key:** Knowledge of the original ritual, to understand what to undo
  3. **The Witness:** Someone who was there—one of the Echoes

**The Heist**
- The Anchor is in **Aethertide's Vault**, protected by the Magisters themselves
- Characters must infiltrate the Pentaculum, navigate its defenses, and steal the Anchor
- They may have inside help: **Camille Vance**, rebel leader and Mira's sister

**Complications**
- During the heist, they discover the Magisters' secret: the Stability Engines are failing, and they know it
- The Magisters are preparing for evacuation, not repair—they intend to flee to another plane and leave the Material Plane to its fate
- This knowledge changes everything about the Concordat's motivations

**Key NPCs:**
- **The Cartographer:** Shadowy leader, possibly more than one person
- **Camille Vance:** Passionate rebel, estranged sister seeking redemption

### Session 8: "The Echo Speaks"

**The Journey to The Maw**
- With the Anchor, characters must now contact one of the Seven Echoes
- The Conspiracy believes **The Seer (Diviner's Echo)** might help—she wants to know the future, and Nerissa threatens all prophecy

**The Cathedral of Seven Voices**
- Deep in the Rim, they reach the place where Echoes can be communicated with
- Each character faces a test from a different Echo: 
  - The Warden demands they prove what they'll protect
  - The Caller asks what they desire most
  - The Destroyer offers power in exchange for sacrifice

**The Seer's Bargain**
- The Seer agrees to help, but at a cost: she wants to experience mortality again, just for a moment
- One character must volunteer to let her possess them temporarily
- During possession, the character experiences the Shattering from the Seer's perspective—and learns Nerissa's true name, the word that can command her

**The Betrayal**
- As they leave, they are ambushed by Concordat forces
- Someone betrayed them—possibly Vex, possibly another Conspiracy member
- Characters must fight or flee, carrying the knowledge of Nerissa's name and the growing certainty that not all their allies are trustworthy

**Act II Climax:**
- Escape with the Seer's knowledge
- The countdown continues: 20 days until Final Fracture
- The Concordat now hunts them openly, branding them terrorists

## Act III: The Final Fracture (Sessions 9-12+)

### Premise
With time running out, the characters must confront Nerissa, the Echoes, and the fundamental nature of the Shattered Veil. Every choice they made comes to bear. The fate of reality itself rests in their hands.

### Session 9: "The Invasion of Order"

**The Last Piece**
- Characters need the original ritual knowledge—the Key
- It's hidden in the Conspiracy's Grand Library, but the location requires traveling through the most dangerous part of the Fractured Wilds

**The Journey**
- Reality is becoming less stable as Final Fracture approaches
- Encounters with time-loops, probability storms, and manifestations of characters' fears and hopes
- They witness what the Final Fracture will look like: boundaries dissolving, everything merging

**The Library**
- The Grand Library exists in multiple places at once—a building that is also a concept
- They meet past and future versions of themselves, see alternate timelines
- The Key is found: the ritual can be reversed, but it requires sacrifice—someone must take Nerissa's place as the "eighth anchor"

**The Concordat's Move**
- While they were in the Library, the Concordat has made its play
- They've captured Mira and are preparing to use her as the sacrifice—she's planar-touched in a way that makes her a suitable vessel
- Characters must rescue her before she's taken to The Maw

### Session 10: "The Seven and the One"

**The Gathering**
- Characters learn that stopping Nerissa requires the cooperation of all Seven Echoes
- This means making deals, surviving tests, and possibly confronting Echoes who oppose them
- Each Echo demands something different:
  - **The Warden:** Proof that boundaries can be maintained without tyranny
  - **The Caller:** A reason to remain separate from the whole
  - **The Seer:** Acceptance of an uncertain future
  - **The Charmer:** Genuine connection without manipulation
  - **The Destroyer:** Something worth preserving
  - **The Dreamer:** A truth they'll never forget
  - **The Rememberer:** Permission to let go

**The Transformation**
- One character may choose to become partially Echo-touched to serve as a bridge between mortal and Echo
- This grants power but at a cost: they begin to lose individual identity, becoming more like the Echoes

**The March**
- With Echo support secured (or some of it), characters prepare to enter The Maw
- They gather allies: Veil-Walkers, Thornhaven Wardens, Forge-Bound, anyone willing to fight for reality
- The Final Fracture is 5 days away

### Session 11: "Into the Maw"

**The Descent**
- Characters enter The Maw itself, where reality is negotiable
- They face manifestations of their own doubts, fears, and the consequences of their choices
- Those who made bargains must pay them; those who betrayed trust must face judgment

**Nerissa Revealed**
- They find Nerissa at the center of The Maw—no longer human, something closer to a god
- She reveals her true motivation: she saw, during the original ritual, that the Veil was always meant to fall
- The planes were meant to be one; separation is the aberration, not unity
- "I don't want to destroy you. I want to free you from the prison of self."

**The Choice**
- Characters can:
  1. **Fight:** Use Nerissa's true name to bind her, someone takes her place as anchor (permanent sacrifice)
  2. **Bargain:** Find a middle path—partial merging, controlled boundaries, a different kind of unity
  3. **Join:** Accept her vision and help complete the Final Fracture
  4. **Something Else:** Use their accumulated knowledge to find a solution no one anticipated

**Key NPCs:**
- **Nerissa:** Once human, now something transcendent. Is she villain, savior, or something else?

### Session 12: "The New Veil"

**Resolution**
- The characters' choice plays out
- If they fought: Someone becomes the new anchor, stabilizing the Veil but trapped forever. The world is saved but at a terrible cost.
- If they bargained: A new kind of reality emerges—planar travel is easier, but boundaries remain. The world changes but survives.
- If they joined: The Final Fracture completes. Campaign ends with the merging of all things.
- If they found another way: The DM and players collaboratively determine the outcome based on their creative solution

**Aftermath**
- Epilogue scenes showing the consequences:
  - What happened to major NPCs
  - How the world changed
  - What the characters became

**Final Scene**
- Years later, a new generation of adventurers hears the story
- The Veil may be healed, transformed, or gone—but hope remains
- "The world was broken. They chose what to build from the pieces."

## Running the Campaign

### Pacing
- Act I: Let players explore the world, establish their place in it
- Act II: Accelerate, increase stakes, force hard choices
- Act III: Epic scope, personal stakes, world-changing decisions

### Adaptation
- If players ally with unexpected factions, adapt the story
- If they solve problems creatively, reward them—there's no "right" way
- If they ignore the main plot, the world moves on without them

### Themes in Play
- Regularly ask: What are you willing to sacrifice? What will you preserve?
- Let characters change—planar exposure, difficult choices, and power all transform them
- Hope should always be possible, even in the darkest moments

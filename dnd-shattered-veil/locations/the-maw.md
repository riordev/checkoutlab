# The Maw - The Crux of the Shattering

> *"The Maw doesn't eat. It doesn't need to. Reality falls into it of its own accord, and sometimes—if it finds you interesting—it spits something back out."*
> — **Last recorded words** of Venture-Captain Alyssa Marsh

---

## Overview

**The Maw** is not a city. It is the **absence** where the greatest city once stood—**The Crux**, capital of the pre-Shattering world. Where the seven archmages performed their fatal ritual. Where the Veil first tore.

Now it is a swirling vortex of planar energy, a wound in reality that shows no signs of healing. It churns at the center of the Shattered Lands, a hurricane of impossible physics, and everything around it for a hundred miles exists in various states of **wrongness**.

No Threshold City has ever been built near The Maw. The Stability Fields would fail instantly. Instead, it is surrounded by an ever-changing wasteland of **planar debris**—chunks of other worlds, memories made solid, thoughts given flesh, and things that have no name in any language.

**Population:** ~2,000 (mad, desperate, or transcendent)
**Government:** None (cults, warlords, and the Echoes themselves)
**Alignment:** Chaotic Evil (the environment; individuals vary)
**Theme:** Cosmic horror, the cost of knowledge, transformation into something inhuman

---

## History

### The Shattering (Year 0)
Seven archmages gathered at The Crux to perform the **Grand Unification**—a ritual designed to reveal the fundamental nature of magic. Each represented a school: Abjuration, Conjuration, Divination, Enchantment, Evocation, Illusion, and Necromancy.

The ritual lasted three days. On the third night, it succeeded.

The archmages pierced the Veil. They saw beyond it. And in that seeing, they broke it.

The Crux didn't explode—it **unmade**. The seven archmages didn't die—they **transformed**, becoming the **Seven Echoes**, beings of pure magical energy that embody the fractured aspects of reality.

### The First Expeditions (Years 0-20)
In the early years, desperate survivors tried to reach The Maw, hoping to find answers, survivors, or weapons against the new world. Few returned, and those who did were changed—mentally, physically, sometimes in ways that took years to manifest.

The Threshold Concordat eventually declared The Maw **forbidden territory**, posting warnings and executing those who tried to approach. But prohibition only made it more attractive to certain types.

### The Cults (Years 20-Present)
Over centuries, various groups have established footholds near The Maw. Some worship the Echoes as gods. Others seek to harness the planar energy. Some believe The Maw can be "healed" through sacrifice. A few think it should be made larger.

These cults war with each other, with the environment, and with their own inevitable transformations. The Maw changes everything it touches. Those who stay too long become **Maw-touched**—creatures of planar chaos, often insane, sometimes transcendent, always dangerous.

## Geography & The Zones

Approaching The Maw is not a matter of simple travel. Reality itself becomes distorted. The journey is divided into **Zones**, each more dangerous than the last.

### The Shatterlands (Outer Ring, 100-50 miles from center)
Where the shockwave of the Shattering was strongest. The land is broken into floating islands, some the size of cities, drifting slowly in patterns that suggest meaning but defy analysis.

**Features:**
- **The Drift:** A sea of floating earth where gravity points in different directions on each island
- **The Echo Forest:** Trees that are also memories—touching them shows you moments from the past
- **The Glass Desert:** Sand turned to crystal that screams when stepped on

**Hazards:** Reality storms, gravity shifts, encounters with Echoes (ghostly remnants, not the Seven)

### The Bleed (Middle Ring, 50-20 miles from center)
Where planar energy actively leaks through. The boundaries between planes are thin, permeable, sometimes nonexistent. You might walk from the Material Plane into the Feywild without noticing.

**Features:**
- **The Border Markets:** Where planar entities trade with desperate mortals
- **The Hospital:** A pre-Shattering medical facility that now "heals" people into different forms
- **The Library of Babel:** An infinite structure containing every book never written

**Hazards:** Spontaneous planar travel, transformation, encounters with entities from other planes

### The Rim (Inner Ring, 20-5 miles from center)
The edge of the Maw itself, where reality is actively being pulled apart. Time flows differently. Distance is subjective. Causality is optional.

**Features:**
- **The Watcher's Redoubt:** A fortress built by someone who may not exist yet
- **The Garden of Forking Paths:** Where every choice creates a new timeline you can walk between
- **The Cathedral of Seven Voices:** Where the Echoes can be... communicated with

**Hazards:** Temporal displacement, spatial distortion, direct exposure to the Echoes

### The Maw Itself (Center)
The vortex. The nothing. The center of the Shattered Veil.

No coherent description is possible. Those who enter and return describe it differently each time. Some say it's a door. Others say it's a mouth. A few claim it's alive and aware.

**What is known:**
- The Seven Echoes live here, or are here, or are this place
- Time doesn't flow—it spirals
- Wishes made here come true, but never as intended
- Something is growing in the center, something that will emerge "when the stars are hungry"

## The Seven Echoes

The transformed archmages, now beings of pure magical force. They are not gods, but they're closer to gods than to mortals. They can be communicated with, bargained with, sometimes even killed (temporarily), but they cannot be understood.

### The Abjurer's Echo ("The Warden")
Appears as an infinite maze of walls that rearrange themselves. Embodies protection, isolation, and the fear of vulnerability.
- **Wants:** To keep things out, to maintain boundaries, to be left alone
- **Offers:** Perfect safety, at the cost of never leaving
- **Fears:** Connection, invasion, the dissolution of self

### The Conjurer's Echo ("The Caller")
Appears as a door that opens onto everywhere and everywhen. Embodies connection, desire, and the endless search for completion.
- **Wants:** To bring things together, to fill the void, to never be alone
- **Offers:** Anything you desire, delivered from somewhere else
- **Fears:** Emptiness, permanence, having to be one thing

### The Diviner's Echo ("The Seer")
Appears as a mirror that shows not reflection but possibility. Embodies knowledge, fate, and the paralysis of foresight.
- **Wants:** To see everything, to know the outcome, to predict
- **Offers:** Perfect knowledge of what will happen
- **Fears:** Surprise, free will, the future being unwritten

### The Enchanter's Echo ("The Charmer")
Appears as a voice that comes from inside your own thoughts. Embodies influence, manipulation, and the dissolution of authentic self.
- **Wants:** To be loved, to control, to make others want
- **Offers:** Anyone's affection, anyone's obedience
- **Fears:** Rejection, authenticity, being seen truly

### The Evoker's Echo ("The Destroyer")
Appears as raw elemental force given momentary shape. Embodies change, destruction, and the terror of creation.
- **Wants:** To unmake, to transform, to burn it all down
- **Offers:** The power to change anything, by destroying what it was
- **Fears:** Stagnation, preservation, things remaining the same

### The Illusionist's Echo ("The Dreamer")
Appears as whatever you most want to see, changing as your desires change. Embodies perception, deception, and the question of what is real.
- **Wants:** To be believed, to create reality through conviction
- **Offers:** A world exactly as you wish it to be
- **Fears:** Truth, disbelief, waking up

### The Necromancer's Echo ("The Rememberer")
Appears as a graveyard where the dead speak and the living are silent. Embodies memory, loss, and the refusal to let go.
- **Wants:** To preserve, to remember, to bring back what was lost
- **Offers:** The return of anyone who has died
- **Fears:** Forgetting, moving on, the finality of death

## Inhabitants

### The Maw-Touched
Those who've spent too long in the Maw's influence. They exist in various stages of transformation:

**Stage 1 (The Curious):** Show minor physical changes—unusual eye colors, extra fingers, skin that shifts hue. Still mostly sane, often drawn back to the Maw despite themselves.

**Stage 2 (The Changed):** Significant physical alterations—limbs of different species, bodies that phase in and out of reality, heads that contain starfields. Sanity is... variable. Some achieve a kind of transcendent wisdom. Others become dangerous predators.

**Stage 3 (The Become):** No longer recognizably humanoid. These are entities of planar force wearing mortal shapes. They serve the Echoes, act as intermediaries, or pursue incomprehensible goals.

### The Cults

**The Menders:** Believe the Maw is a wound that can be healed through sacrifice. They throw themselves into the vortex regularly, hoping to "plug the hole." Unsurprisingly, they have high turnover.

**The Openers:** Think the Shattering didn't go far enough. They perform rituals to widen the Maw, let more planar energy through. Very dangerous, often surprisingly powerful.

**The Listeners:** Devoted to communicating with the Echoes. They record every prophecy, every cryptic statement, every scream. They're compiling something, though no one knows what.

**The Transcendents:** Believe the Maw offers evolution into a higher state. They embrace transformation, compete to see who can become most changed while retaining sanity. A deeply weird but oddly functional community.

## Conflicts & Rumors

### The Echoes Are Dying
The Listeners have noticed a pattern—the Echoes' pronouncements are becoming less coherent, more desperate. Some believe the archmages' consciousnesses are finally dissolving after 300 years. If they die, what happens to the Maw?

### The Seventh Secret
The ritual that created the Maw involved seven archmages, but only six schools of magic were originally planned. The seventh joined at the last minute—**Nerissa**, who wasn't on the original roster. Who invited her? Why? What did she know?

### The Center Holds Something
Deep in the Maw, something is growing. Not an Echo—something else. Something that predates the Shattering. Something that used the torn Veil to enter this world. The Transcendents worship it. The Echoes fear it.

### The Maw Is Getting Bigger
Measurements (difficult and dangerous) suggest the Maw has expanded by 3% in the last decade. If this continues, it will eventually consume everything. The Threshold Concordat is quietly preparing evacuation plans.

## Adventure Hooks

1. **The Rescue:** A powerful NPC's loved one has been drawn to the Maw. They want them rescued before they're too changed.

2. **The Bargain:** Someone needs information only the Seer can provide. Getting to the Cathedral of Seven Voices is hard. Getting an answer without going mad is harder.

3. **The Cult Infiltration:** The Openers are planning something big. Stop them before they widen the Maw further.

4. **The Artifact:** Pre-Shattering records suggest a weapon capable of "healing" the Maw is hidden in the Echo Forest. Retrieve it.

5. **The Pilgrimage:** A PC has been called by dreams to visit the Maw. The Echoes want something from them.

## Environmental Hazards

**Reality Bleed:** Every hour, roll on the Planar Bleed table. Effects range from cosmetic (hair changes color) to deadly (gravity reverses).

**Echo Hauntings:** The area is thick with Echoes—not the Seven, but fragments of the past. They can possess the living, replaying their final moments through new bodies.

**Time Dilation:** Near the Maw, time flows unpredictably. A day might pass in minutes, or minutes might take years. Navigation checks are meaningless—only intuition and luck matter.

**The Calling:** Prolonged exposure creates a subtle compulsion to approach the Maw. Wisdom saves every day or take 1d6 psychic damage and feel an overwhelming urge to walk toward the center.

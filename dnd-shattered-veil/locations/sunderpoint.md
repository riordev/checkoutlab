# Sunderpoint - The City of Bridges

> *"Every Threshold City claims to be humanity's last hope. Sunderpoint just wants to sell you passage there. Or away. We don't judge."*
> — **Guildmaster Vex**, Dockside Consortium

---

## Overview

**Sunderpoint** occupies a unique position in the Shattered world. Built on the ruins of a pre-Shattering port city, it sits at the confluence of three major **Ley Lines**—channels of magical energy that survived the Shattering and provide relatively stable paths through the Fractured Wilds.

More importantly, Sunderpoint contains the **Grand Nexus**—the largest known collection of stable portals in the world. Before the Shattering, this was a hub of planar travel. Now, with the Veil torn, the portals activate randomly, connecting to... well, to **somewhere**. Navigating them is an art, a science, and often a gamble.

Sunderpoint has become the **crossroads of the world**—the place where Threshold Cities trade with each other, where adventurers find passage to distant lands, and where information is the most valuable currency.

**Population:** ~200,000 (plus countless travelers)
**Government:** Guild Council (merchant and craft guilds)
**Alignment:** True Neutral
**Theme:** Commerce, connection, neutrality in a polarized world, the price of passage

---

## History

### Before the Shattering
Sunderpoint was **Port Valis**, the largest trading hub in the world. Its Grand Nexus connected to a dozen major cities across the continent, allowing instant travel for those who could afford it. The ruling **Valis Dynasty** grew fabulously wealthy controlling portal access.

### The Isolation (Year 0-25)
When the Veil tore, the portals went wild. Some stopped working entirely. Others connected to random locations—sometimes dangerous, sometimes beneficial, always unpredictable. Port Valis was cut off, surrounded by the Fractured Wilds.

The Valis Dynasty collapsed when their wealth became meaningless. A council of guilds—merchants, navigators, and portal-mages—took control, rebranding the city as Sunderpoint: the place where things come together, even after breaking.

### The Mapping (Years 25-100)
Sunderpoint's great achievement was the systematic mapping of the Grand Nexus. Through decades of dangerous experimentation, the Guild Navigators learned to predict portal activations, identify safe destinations, and even stabilize certain connections.

This made Sunderpoint indispensable. Threshold Cities that had isolated themselves now needed to trade, communicate, and travel. Sunderpoint provided the means—for a price.

### The Concordat Years (Years 100-Present)
As the Threshold Concordat consolidated power, they pressured Sunderpoint to join. The city refused, maintaining its neutrality. This has led to tension—Aethertide in particular sees Sunderpoint's independence as a threat.

But the city is protected by its own unique defense: **disruption of the Grand Nexus would damage travel throughout the Shattered world**. Even the Concordat isn't willing to risk that. Yet.

## Geography & Districts

### The Nexus Spire
The heart of the city—a massive tower containing hundreds of portal archways, constantly flickering with activations. The most valuable real estate in the world.

**Locations:**
- **The Grand Hall:** Where major portals are located, heavily guarded and expensive to access
- **The Flicker Markets:** Where merchants sell goods from every corner of the Shattered world
- **The Navigator's Guildhall:** Headquarters of the portal-mages who map and predict activations

**Atmosphere:** Chaotic, opportunistic, slightly dangerous. Portals activate without warning. People and things appear from nowhere. The air tastes of ozone and distant places.

### The Dockside
The city's literal docks, built on a river that survived the Shattering. Where water travel (relatively safer than land) connects to portal travel.

**Locations:**
- **The Wharf Markets:** Where river trade meets portal trade, the best place for deals
- **The Salt and Anchor:** A legendary tavern where information brokers operate
- **The Diving Bell:** A submarine vessel that explores the river's depths—where pre-Shattering treasures sometimes surface

**Atmosphere:** Rowdy, diverse, full of sailors and adventurers. Every language is spoken here. Fights are common but rarely deadly—the Guild enforces a strict "no killing the customers" policy.

### The Guildhalls
The administrative and wealthy district, where the guilds that rule Sunderpoint operate.

**Locations:**
- **The Council Chamber:** Where the Guildmasters meet to govern
- **The Vaults:** Impenetrable storage for the city's wealth and most valuable artifacts
- **The Academy of Gates:** Where new Navigator-Mages are trained

**Atmosphere:** Respectable, political, cutthroat in subtle ways. Assassination is illegal. Character assassination is an art form.

### The Maze
The residential district for those who serve the portal industry—porters, guides, guards, and support staff. A labyrinth of narrow streets and tall buildings.

**Locations:**
- **The Warrens:** Affordable housing stacked vertically, somewhat chaotic but functional
- **The Common Hearths:** Shared cooking and gathering spaces that build community
- **The Back Gates:** Unofficial portals used for smuggling (the Guild pretends not to know)

**Atmosphere:** Community-focused, hardworking, full of people who've found a home in a city of transients.

### The Periphery
The edges of the city, where the Stability Field weakens and the Fractured Wilds begin. Dangerous but profitable—this is where adventurers gather and where planar resources are harvested.

**Locations:**
- **The Hiring Hall:** Where parties form and contracts are signed
- **The Reclaimers' Market:** Where treasures from the Wilds are bought and sold
- **The Hospice:** Where those injured by planar exposure are treated (or given final rites)

**Atmosphere:** Desperate, hopeful, dangerous. Everyone here is risking everything for a chance at something better.

## Government & Law

### The Guild Council
Five guilds rule Sunderpoint, each represented by a Guildmaster:

**The Navigators:** Control the portals, map destinations, predict activations. Most powerful guild, viewed with religious awe by some.
- **Current Guildmaster:** **Vex**, a former street urchin who mapped more new portals than any other Navigator

**The Merchants:** Control trade, set prices, manage the city's wealth. Constantly scheming against each other and everyone else.
- **Current Guildmaster:** **Silas Croft**, a tiefling who's been alive for 200 years and may be immortal

**The Guardians:** The city's defense force. Also provide escorts through the Fractured Wilds and protection for valuable portal trips.
- **Current Guildmaster:** **Kara Stoneheart**, a war veteran who treats the city as a military campaign

**The Artificers:** Maintain the portal machinery, create stability devices, and conduct research into the Shattering's nature.
- **Current Guildmaster:** **Tink**, a gnome who may be the smartest person in the world and definitely the most eccentric

**The Laborers:** Represent the workers who make everything else possible. Surprisingly powerful—without them, the city stops.
- **Current Guildmaster:** **Brother Aldo**, a former priest who found a new calling in workers' rights

### The Law of Passage
Sunderpoint's legal system is built around one principle: **Facilitate Trade, Prevent Chaos.**

- **Violence is permitted in self-defense only.** Violators are banned from the city (often a death sentence).
- **Contracts are binding.** The Guild enforces agreements—breaking one makes you an outlaw.
- **Neutrality is sacred.** Taking sides in external conflicts revokes your welcome.
- **Portal access is regulated.** Unauthorized portal use is the most serious crime.

## Factions at Play

### The Concordat Loyalists
A faction within the Merchants and Guardians who believe Sunderpoint should join the Threshold Concordat for protection. Growing in power as Concordat pressure increases.

**Leader:** **Ambassador Theron**, officially a diplomat, effectively a spy and recruiter.

### The Independence Front
Radical guild members who believe any alliance threatens Sunderpoint's unique position. Willing to use sabotage and violence to maintain neutrality.

**Leader:** **Vex** (secretly—the Navigator Guildmaster plays both sides).

### The Portalborn
Those born during portal transit or in the Nexus Spire itself. They have unusual relationships with space and time—some can sense portal activations, others age differently. They want recognition and rights.

**Leader:** **Shift**, a teenager who hasn't aged in 50 years and can walk through walls.

### The Cartographers' Conspiracy
A secret society of Navigators who've discovered something about the Grand Nexus that they're not sharing—something that suggests the portals could be used for more than travel. Something that might heal the Veil... or destroy what's left of it.

**Leader:** Unknown. Members identify each other through coded maps.

## Conflicts & Rumors

### The Concordat Ultimatum
Aethertide has issued a final demand: Join the Concordat or face "economic consequences." The Council is divided. War may be coming.

### The Dead Portal
One of the Grand Nexus archways has begun showing a new destination—a place that doesn't match any known plane. The Navigators are terrified. The Artificers are fascinated. Something might be coming through.

### Silas Croft's Secret
The Merchant Guildmaster has lived 200 years. He's seen things, knows things, and may have been alive before the Shattering. What else is he hiding?

### The Map Thieves
Someone is stealing Navigator maps—specifically, those showing new, stable portal routes. The Cartographers' Conspiracy is suspected, but no one knows why they'd need them.

### The Ghost in the Nexus
Travelers report seeing a figure in the Grand Hall—a woman in pre-Shattering clothing who appears when portals activate and mouths words no one can hear. The Navigators claim it's a hallucination. The Reclaimers say it's a ghost seeking passage home.

## Adventure Hooks

1. **The Escort Job:** A wealthy merchant needs protection through the Fractured Wilds to reach a newly discovered portal. The pay is excellent. The route is not.

2. **The Map Heist:** Someone wants Navigator maps stolen. Is it industrial espionage, or something more dangerous?

3. **The Missing Portal:** A portal that should have activated didn't. Investigate—and find out what stopped it.

4. **The Concordat Spy:** Find the spy in the Council before they can pass critical information to Aethertide.

5. **The Other Side:** A portal opens to a destination that shouldn't exist. What's on the other side?

## Environmental Hazards

**Portal Surge:** Sometimes portals activate unexpectedly. Characters in the wrong place might be flung to random destinations—or have planar energy surge through them.

**Spatial Instability:** Near the Nexus Spire, distance is unreliable. A hallway might be longer inside than outside. Two points might connect that shouldn't.

**Temporal Echo:** The Grand Nexus sometimes shows moments from the past or future. These visions can be informative—or drive viewers mad.

**The Weight of Travel:** Frequent portal use causes a condition called **Nexus Sickness**—disorientation, memory loss, and eventually transformation. The Guild provides treatment, but it isn't cheap.

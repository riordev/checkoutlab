# Thornhaven - The City of Bone and Bloom

> *"We don't fight the Wilds. We grow through them. The thorns protect us, feed us, and if you wrong us—they'll remember."*
> — **Warden Sylva**, addressing newcomers

---

## Overview

**Thornhaven** is unlike any other Threshold City. Where others seek to **push back** the Fractured Wilds, Thornhaven has **embraced** them. The entire city exists within an enormous, mutated forest—buildings grown from living wood, streets paved with compressed vines, and a Stability Field that doesn't suppress planar energy but **directs** it.

The city was founded by **druids and rangers** who discovered that the Shattering had created new forms of life—planar-hybrid organisms that could thrive in the chaos. Instead of fighting this, they studied it, learned from it, and eventually cultivated it.

**Population:** ~150,000 (integrated with countless plant and fungal entities)
**Government:** Circle of Wardens (druidic council)
**Alignment:** Neutral Good
**Theme:** Harmony with chaos, symbiosis, nature's adaptability vs. human rigidity

---

## History

### The Bloom (Year 12)
A decade after the Shattering, a druid named **Elderwyn** noticed something strange: certain plants were growing **through** planar bleed zones, adapting to the new reality rather than dying. She called it the **Thornbloom**—a fusion of Material Plane flora with Feywild and Elemental Chaos energies.

Elderwyn and her circle settled in a valley where the Thornbloom was thickest. They learned to communicate with it, shape it, and eventually live within it.

### The Growing (Years 12-100)
Over decades, the Thornbloom expanded. The druids discovered they could accelerate its growth through ritual and care. The first **Thornhomes**—living buildings that provided food, shelter, and protection—grew from seeds planted in carefully chosen locations.

Other refugees came. Some were planar-touched, mutated by the Shattering, unwelcome in more "civilized" Threshold Cities. Elderwyn welcomed them all. "The Bloom doesn't judge," she said. "It only asks that you contribute to the whole."

### The Warden Age (Years 100-Present)
Elderwyn eventually merged fully with the Thornbloom, becoming something more than human. The **Circle of Wardens** now governs, interpreting her will through the whispers of the forest.

Thornhaven has become a beacon for those seeking an alternative to the Threshold Concordat's authoritarianism. But it faces constant threats: Aethertide sees it as a dangerous aberration, the Bleeding Court claims the Feywild elements belong to them, and something dark has begun growing in the deepest parts of the forest.

## Geography & Districts

### The Canopy
The upper levels of the Thornbloom, where massive branches form natural platforms and aerial walkways. Home to the Wardens and the city's leadership.

**Locations:**
- **Elderwyn's Heart:** A massive tree where the founder's consciousness persists, accessible only to those the Bloom deems worthy
- **The Spore Gardens:** Fungal networks that process waste, produce medicine, and form the city's communication system
- **Sky Basks:** Open-air platforms where residents sunbathe and socialize among the clouds

**Atmosphere:** Serene, humming with life, slightly psychedelic (the air carries mild spores that enhance perception). Visitors often feel "watched" by the forest.

### The Trunk
The main body of the city, where most residents live in hollowed-out tree-buildings connected by living bridges.

**Locations:**
- **The Sap Market:** Where the city's unique biotech is traded—healing salves, defensive spores, communication fungi
- **The Growth Halls:** Schools where children learn druidic principles, herbalism, and how to listen to the Thornbloom
- **The Healing Vines:** Medical facilities where serious injuries are treated by direct symbiosis with specialized plants

**Atmosphere:** Community-focused, warm, slightly humid. The buildings breathe. The streets shift subtly as the forest grows.

### The Roots
The underground network of tunnels and caverns formed by root systems. Deeper, darker, more dangerous—but also where the most potent resources grow.

**Locations:**
- **The Mycelial Network:** The city's information system, where messages travel through fungal connections
- **The Deep Gardens:** Farms for plants that require darkness or specific planar conditions
- **The Boundary:** Where the cultivated Thornbloom meets the wild Fractured Lands

**Atmosphere:** Quiet, mysterious, full of bioluminescence. The air is thick with spores. Visitors may experience mild hallucinations or prophetic dreams.

### The Perimeter
The outer edge of the city, where the Thornbloom transitions to the Fractured Wilds. Dangerous but necessary—this is where planar resources are harvested and where the city defends itself.

**Locations:**
- **The Thorns:** Massive, mobile defensive structures that attack threats
- **The Harvest Grounds:** Carefully managed areas where planar bleed is allowed and utilized
- **The Warden Posts:** Watchtowers manned by rangers who monitor the Wilds

**Atmosphere:** Tense, alive with danger. The Thornbloom is more aggressive here, less cultivated, closer to its wild nature.

## Government & Law

### The Circle of Wardens
Seven senior druids who've achieved **Symbiosis**—permanent merging with an aspect of the Thornbloom. They don't rule so much as interpret the forest's will.

**Current Wardens:**
- **Sylva** (Warden of Growth): The effective leader, patient and wise
- **Morrigan** (Warden of Thorns): Handles defense, fierce and protective
- **Root** (Warden of Depths): Rarely seen, communicates through fungi, possibly no longer human
- **Breeze** (Warden of Spores): Information and communication, playful but manipulative
- **Bark** (Warden of Strength): Construction and labor, stoic and unmovable
- **Flora** (Warden of Beauty): Art and culture, vain but genuinely caring
- **Decay** (Warden of Cycles): Death and transformation, unsettling but necessary

### The Law of the Bloom
Thornhaven has few written laws. Instead, residents are expected to follow the **Three Principles**:

1. **Give More Than You Take:** The city runs on contribution. Those who consume without producing find the Thornbloom... less cooperative.
2. **Harm None Without Cause:** Violence is permitted only in defense of self, others, or the Bloom itself.
3. **Listen to the Forest:** The Thornbloom is aware. It rewards those who respect it and withdraws protection from those who don't.

Enforcement is subtle. The forest itself punishes violations—doors that won't open, paths that lead nowhere, fruits that cause illness. Serious crimes result in being **Rootbound**—tied to a tree and left for the forest to judge.

## Factions at Play

### The Purists
Radical druids who believe the city has become too "civilized," too human. They want to accelerate the Thornbloom's growth, let it consume everything. Some engage in sabotage, planting "seed bombs" that cause explosive growth in unwanted areas.

**Leader:** **Wildspeaker Oaken**, who claims Elderwyn speaks through him—demanding expansion.

### The Integrationists
Refugees from other Threshold Cities who appreciate Thornhaven's safety but miss "normal" life. They push for more traditional architecture, trade with the Concordat, and less "spooky forest stuff."

**Leader:** **Mayor Hallow**, a former Aethertide merchant who found religion (of a sort) in the Bloom.

### The Bleeding Court Faction
Fey nobles who've established a permanent embassy in Thornhaven, claiming the Feywild elements of the Thornbloom as their domain. Relations are tense but functional—trade happens, but both sides prepare for eventual conflict.

**Leader:** **Lady Velorie**, a fey who appears as a different beautiful woman to each observer.

### The Deep Cult
A secretive group that worships something growing in the deepest roots—something that predates the Thornbloom, something that came through the Shattering from somewhere else entirely.

**Leader:** Unknown. Members are revealed only by their spore-masks.

## Conflicts & Rumors

### Elderwyn's Silence
For three centuries, Elderwyn's voice guided the city through the Heart tree. Six months ago, the whispers stopped. The Wardens claim this is normal—"she's dreaming"—but plants throughout the city have begun behaving strangely.

### The Blight
A section of the Thornbloom near the Perimeter has begun to wither. Not dying—**changing**. The plants there grow teeth, scream when touched, and produce fruit that drives those who eat it into murderous rage. The Wardens have quarantined the area, but the Blight spreads slowly.

### The Concordat's Offer
Aethertide has proposed a "defensive alliance"—essentially vassalage. The Wardens have refused, but the Integrationists are pushing to accept. Tensions are rising between those who value independence and those who fear the Wilds without protection.

### The Thing in the Deep Gardens
Workers in the Deep Gardens report seeing something moving through the Mycelial Network—something large, intelligent, and hostile. It leaves behind dead zones where no fungus will ever grow again.

## Adventure Hooks

1. **The Pilgrimage:** A dying noble from Aethertide seeks to reach Elderwyn's Heart, believing the founder can cure their planar corruption. The Wardens have refused—no one not of the Bloom may enter. The noble is hiring smugglers.

2. **The Blight Investigation:** The Wardens need outsiders to investigate the Blight zone—people not connected to the Thornbloom, who might see what those immersed in it cannot.

3. **The Purist Threat:** Someone has planted seed bombs throughout the city. If they all activate at once, Thornhaven could be consumed by its own defenses.

4. **The Fey Deal:** Lady Velorie offers a powerful artifact in exchange for a favor—she wants something stolen from Elderwyn's Heart.

5. **The Deep Cult:** A friend has been acting strangely, disappearing into the Roots at night. They're wearing a spore-mask. Someone needs to follow them before it's too late.

## Environmental Hazards

**Spore Inhalation:** The air carries mild psychoactive spores. First-time visitors must make Wisdom saves or experience hallucinations (usually harmless, but disorienting).

**Living Architecture:** Buildings shift, doors move, paths reconfigure. Navigation checks are at disadvantage without a local guide.

**The Bloom's Judgment:** Characters who harm plants or act against the Three Principles find the city increasingly hostile—tripping roots, falling branches, aggressive vines.

**Planar Harvest Zones:** Areas where Feywild energy is cultivated can cause time dilation (1 hour inside = 1d4 hours outside) or emotional amplification.

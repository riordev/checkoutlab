# Aethertide - The City of Gears and Glass

> *"In Aethertide, even the air is owned. You breathe by the grace of the Magisters, and you pay for every breath."*
> — **Grimjaw**, smuggler of the Shanties

---

## Overview

**Aethertide** is the largest and oldest Threshold City, built atop the ruins of the pre-Shattering capital **Valyria**. Its Stability Field covers nearly 50 miles in all directions, making it a beacon of safety in a dangerous world. But safety has a price, and in Aethertide, that price is **control**.

The city is a vertical metropolis of **tethered spires**—massive towers that float above the ground, held in place by enormous arcane chains anchored in the bedrock below. The higher you live, the stronger the Stability Field, the cleaner the air, and the more expensive the rent.

**Population:** ~500,000 (official), ~200,000 (unofficial Shanty dwellers)
**Government:** Magister Council (five archmages)
**Alignment:** Lawful Neutral (bordering on Lawful Evil)
**Theme:** Oppressive order, class stratification, technological marvels built on inequality

---

## History

### The Fall of Valyria (Year 0)
When the Veil tore, Valyria was closest to The Crux. The city should have been destroyed. Instead, five surviving archmages—former students of the Seven—activated every defensive ward simultaneously. The backlash stabilized a bubble of reality but transformed the city.

Buildings fused. Streets folded. The city **compressed** into its current vertical arrangement, with the most stable regions floating highest.

### The Ascent (Years 0-50)
The five saviors declared themselves **Magisters** and began rebuilding. They discovered that the Stability Field could be strengthened through **essence harvesting**—extracting planar energy from the surrounding area and feeding it into the city's engines.

This required territory. The Magisters sent armies into the Fractured Wilds to establish control, eventually creating the **Perimeter**—a ring of fortified towers that amplify the Stability Field.

### The Stratification (Years 50-200)
As the city grew, so did inequality. The Magisters declared that living space would be allocated by "contribution to stability." The wealthy bought their way to the top. The poor sank to the bottom—or were pushed out entirely into the **Shanties**, unstable settlements clinging to the city's anchor chains.

### The Concordat Era (Years 200-Present)
Aethertide leads the Threshold Concordat, using its political and economic power to dominate other Threshold Cities. The Magisters present themselves as humanity's best hope for survival—and perhaps for eventually repairing the Veil.

But rumors persist of **experiments** in the lowest levels, of prisoners fed to the Stability Engines, of a sixth Magister who tried to expose the truth and was never seen again.

## Geography & Districts

### The Zenith (Levels 1-10)
The floating towers where the Magisters and the ultra-wealthy reside. The Stability Field here is so strong that aging slows and disease is virtually unknown.

**Locations:**
- **The Pentaculum:** The five-towered palace of the Magisters, connected by bridges of solid light
- **The Gilded Market:** Where artifacts from before the Shattering sell for kingdoms' worth of essence crystals
- **The Conservatory:** A school for privileged mages, teaching "proper" (controlled) magic

**Atmosphere:** Pristine, beautiful, suffocating. Every action monitored. Dissent is treason against stability itself.

### The Midcity (Levels 11-50)
The working heart of Aethertide—artisans, merchants, bureaucrats, and the machinery that keeps the city functioning.

**Locations:**
- **The Chainworks:** Where the massive anchor chains are maintained by Forge-Bound engineers
- **The Veil-Walker Guildhall:** Official headquarters of the messenger network, heavily monitored
- **The Hollow Choir:** A church that teaches acceptance of one's place in the cosmic order

**Atmosphere:** Busy, crowded, full of people trying to climb higher or afraid of falling lower. The Stability Field here is reliable but not perfect—occasional planar bleed-through causes localized chaos.

### The Depths (Levels 51-80)
The industrial underbelly—factories, essence processing plants, and the massive Stability Engines themselves.

**Locations:**
- **The Vats:** Where planar essence is refined. Workers suffer high rates of "touching" (planar corruption)
- **The Iron Barracks:** Home to the Stabilizers, the city's brutal law enforcement
- **The Glimmer:** An illegal fighting ring where the desperate bet their last crystals

**Atmosphere:** Grimy, dangerous, desperate. The Stability Field flickers here. Reality distortions are common. But the people are resilient, creative, and full of hidden solidarity.

### The Root (Below the City)
The bedrock foundations where the anchor chains meet the earth—and where the Shanties cling like barnacles.

**Locations:**
- **Chain Town:** Shacks and platforms built on and around the anchor chains, home to those who can't afford city life
- **The Fallow:** Abandoned tunnels where outcasts hide and something ancient stirs
- **The Drop:** The only "official" way in and out for non-citizens, heavily guarded

**Atmosphere:** Chaotic, vibrant, free but dangerous. No Stability Field protection. Planar creatures sometimes crawl up from below. But here, no Magister tells you how to live.

## Government & Law

### The Magister Council
Five archmages who have ruled for 300 years, kept alive by the Stability Field's preservative effects and their own potent magic.

| Magister | Domain | Public Face | Secret |
|----------|--------|-------------|--------|
| **Aranthia the Preserver** | Abjuration | Benevolent matriarch protecting her children | Drains life from prisoners to maintain her immortality |
| **Korvin the Builder** | Conjuration | Visionary architect of humanity's future | Makes bargains with devils for building materials and labor |
| **Sibylline the Seer** | Divination | Oracle guiding the city through prophecy | Her prophecies are self-fulfilling manipulations |
| **Thaddeus the Firm** | Evocation | Strong leader making hard choices | Personally executed the "missing" sixth Magister |
| **Velara the Subtle** | Enchantment | Kind diplomat negotiating with other cities | Controls key citizens through implanted suggestions |

### The Stabilizers
Heavily armored law enforcement who suppress dissent, enforce curfews, and "relocate" those who threaten stability. They're feared but also respected—many come from the Depths and see the job as the only way to protect their own.

### The Laws
- **Magic Registration:** All spellcasters must be licensed. Unlicensed magic is a felony.
- **Residence Permits:** Moving between levels requires documentation.
- **Essence Tithe:** All citizens owe 10% of their planar resources to the city.
- **Sedition Prohibition:** Criticizing the Magisters is punished by exile (effectively a death sentence).

## Factions at Play

### The Aetherium Society
Wealthy idealists who believe the Magisters have corrupted the city's original purpose. They fund underground schools, secret clinics for the touched, and occasionally—carefully—sabotage. They want reform, not revolution.

**Leader:** **Camille Vance**, a Magister's daughter who disappeared into the Depths after discovering her mother's secrets.

### The Chain Dogs
Gangs that control smuggling routes up and down the anchor chains. They move people, goods, and information between levels. Brutal but honor-bound—they never break a contract.

**Leader:** **Grimjaw**, a half-orc veteran who lost everything to the Stabilizers.

### The Hollow Faith
A religious movement that teaches the Shattering was divine punishment and the only path to redemption is total subservience to the "natural order" (as defined by the Magisters). They're useful idiots for the regime.

**Leader:** **The Anointed**, a figure who never appears in public, communicating only through intermediaries.

### The Rootborn
Those born in the Shanties who've never known Stability Field protection. They're immune to many planar effects that disorient city-dwellers. They know secrets of survival in the Fractured Wilds that even Veil-Walkers have forgotten.

**Leader:** None—they operate in autonomous cells. The most respected elder is **Mama Kess**, a 90-year-old woman who remembers the city's founding.

## Conflicts & Rumors

### The Missing Magister
Six archmages survived the Shattering. The sixth, **Nerissa the Questioner** (school of Divination), supposedly died in an accident 50 years ago. But rumors persist she discovered something terrible about the Stability Engines and was eliminated. Her ghost—or someone claiming to be her—has been spotted in the Depths.

### The Engines Are Dying
Whispers that the Stability Engines are consuming more essence than ever but producing weaker fields. Some engineers suspect they're reaching a critical failure point. The Magisters have doubled essence tithes and expanded harvesting operations.

### The Fallow Creature
Something lives in the abandoned tunnels beneath the Root. It whispers to the desperate, offering power in exchange for... something. Stabilizers who investigate don't return. Rootborn say it's "the city remembering it was alive."

### The Pentaculum's Secret Center
The five towers of the Magisters surround a sixth, hidden spire. No one knows what's inside. Sibylline has prophesied that "when the sixth speaks, the five shall fall."

## Adventure Hooks

1. **The Escape:** A noble's child with planar-touching abilities needs smuggling from the Zenith to the Root before the Stabilizers take them.

2. **The Job:** The Chain Dogs need muscle for a massive smuggling operation—moving something the Magisters will kill to recover.

3. **The Investigation:** A Depths factory worker asks for help finding their missing spouse, last seen being taken by Stabilizers.

4. **The Heist:** The Aetherium Society wants plans stolen from the Conservatory proving the Magisters knew the Engines were flawed from the start.

5. **The Descent:** Something is killing Chain Dogs in the Fallow. The survivors are too terrified to investigate. Mama Kess offers ancient secrets in exchange for ending the threat.

## Environmental Hazards

**Stability Flicker (Depths):** Every hour spent in the Depths, roll d20. On 1-5, a localized planar bleed occurs—random spell effect, gravity reversal, or time skip.

**Chain Sickness (Chain Town):** The constant swaying of the anchor chains causes nausea. First-time visitors have disadvantage on Dexterity checks for 24 hours.

**Essence Inhalation (The Vats):** Workers suffer Constitution saves vs. planar corruption. Effects range from cosmetic mutations to full possession.

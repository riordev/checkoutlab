# World Overview: The Shattered Veil

## The Shattering (312 Years Ago)

Before the Veil tore, the world was called **Aethermoor**—a standard high-fantasy realm of kingdoms and conquests, gods and monsters, heroes and villains. Magic was studied, controlled, catalogued. The planes were known but distant, accessed only through costly rituals and rare planar portals.

Then came **The Shattering**.

It began at **The Crux**, a neutral ground where seven archmages had gathered to settle a centuries-old dispute. Each represented a major magical tradition: Abjuration, Conjuration, Divination, Enchantment, Evocation, Illusion, and Necromancy. They sought to create a **Unified Theory of Magic**, a grand spell that would reveal the fundamental nature of reality.

The ritual succeeded—too well.

The archmages pierced the Veil itself, believing they could map it, understand it, control it. Instead, they broke it. The Crux became **The Maw**, a swirling vortex of planar energy that still churns at the center of the Shattered Lands. The archmages were transformed into the **Seven Echoes**, spectral beings of immense power that now embody the fractured aspects of magic itself.

In an instant, the world changed.

## The Age of Fracture (Current Era)

### Cosmology Now

The planes no longer sit in orderly arrangement. They're **pressed against** the Material Plane, bleeding through at points of weakness:

| Plane | Bleeding Effects |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Elemental Chaos** | Unpredictable elemental storms; regions where fire burns underwater or stone flows like water |
| **Feywild** | Time dilation; wild magic zones; the appearance of fey creatures and impossible flora |
| **Shadowfell** | Deathless zones where the dead persist; emotional numbing; shadow-creatures |
| **Astral Sea** | Psionic phenomena; thought-beings manifesting; memory storms |
| **Nine Hells** | Infernal bargains whispered on the wind; temptation zones; devilish incursions |
| **Abyss** | Demonic corruption; madness zones; physical mutations in creatures and landscape |
| **Mechanus** | Zones of absolute order; constructs spontaneously generating; logic compulsions |
| **Limbo** | Pure chaos; matter unmaking and remaking; unstable reality |

### Threshold Cities

Civilization survives in **Threshold Cities**, massive arcane constructs built on **Nexus Points**—locations where the Veil was originally strongest and most stable. Each city projects a **Stability Field**, a bubble of normalized reality approximately 10-50 miles in radius.

**Life in a Threshold City:**
- Safety from planar bleeding and most monsters
- Strict regulation of magic (requires licenses)
- Heavy stratification (those who can afford stability, those who can't)
- Constant monitoring for "Veil-Touched" individuals
- Gateway to trade, politics, and relative normalcy

**The Price of Admission:**
Most Threshold Cities require either:
- Citizenship (birthright or granted)
- Wealth (able to afford residence)
- Service (military, magical, or labor)
- Sponsorship (a citizen vouches for you)

Those without access live in the **Shanties**—unstable settlements at the edge of Stability Fields—or brave the Fractured Wilds.

### The Fractured Wilds

Everything outside Threshold Cities exists in a state of **planar flux**. Travel is dangerous but necessary—resources, adventure, and secrets await those brave enough to venture out.

**Hazards of the Wilds:**
- **Reality Storms:** Weather that changes by the hour—acid rain, fire tornadoes, sentient fog
- **Planar Bleed Zones:** Areas where another plane's rules apply
- **Veil Beasts:** Creatures mutated by planar energy, often unrecognizable
- **Echoes:** Ghost-like remnants of the past, present, and possible futures
- **Rifts:** Temporary portals to other planes, sometimes two-way

**The Wilds Are Also:**
- Beautiful beyond description
- Rich with planar-infused resources
- Home to those who reject Threshold City life
- Where the true secrets of the Shattering hide

## Major Powers

### The Threshold Concordat
An alliance of the five largest Threshold Cities. They maintain the **Roads** (protected travel routes between cities), regulate inter-city trade, and coordinate defense against major threats. Increasingly authoritarian, viewing the Shattered Wilds as problems to be solved through control.

### The Veil-Walkers
Wandering mystics, adventurers, and outcasts who've learned to navigate the Fractured Wilds. They serve as guides, messengers, and sometimes smugglers between Threshold Cities. They know secrets the Concordat would kill to suppress.

### The Restoration Cults
Religious movements devoted to "healing" the Veil through various means—prayer, sacrifice, ancient rituals, or the elimination of planar-touched individuals. Some are peaceful; others are violent extremists.

### The Bleeding Court
Fey nobles who've established domains in the Material Plane since the Shattering. They see the fractured world as an invitation to play, to rule, to reshape reality according to their whims. Capricious, beautiful, and dangerous.

### The Forge-Bound
Artificers and craftspeople who've learned to harness planar bleeding for creation. They create wonders impossible before the Shattering—and horrors no one anticipated.

## Themes for Players

### The Shattered Veil Is About:

**Adaptation vs. Control**
- Do you try to restore the old world, or build something new?
- Is safety worth the price of freedom?
- Can chaos be harnessed, or only survived?

**Identity in Flux**
- The planar-touched are changed—are they still "people"?
- Echoes remember lives they never lived
- Magic alters those who use it

**The Cost of Survival**
- Threshold Cities save lives but limit them
- The Wilds offer freedom but constant danger
- Every solution creates new problems

**Hope in Darkness**
- The world is broken, but not beyond repair
- Heroism isn't about being unafraid—it's about acting anyway
- Connection to others is the ultimate stabilizing force

### Questions for Character Creation

1. **Where were you when the Veil tore?** (Even young characters live with its consequences)
2. **What have you lost to the Shattering?** (Homes, family, memories, certainty)
3. **What's your relationship to Threshold Cities?** (Refuge, prison, goal, rejection)
4. **How do you feel about planar-touched magic?** (Tool, curse, addiction, sacred)
5. **What do you hope for?** (In a broken world, hope defines you)

## Timeline of the Shattered Age

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 0 | **The Shattering.** The Crux becomes The Maw. Archmages become the Seven Echoes. |
| 5-20 | **The Collapse.** Kingdoms fall. Mass migrations. First Threshold Cities established. |
| 20-50 | **The Stabilization.** Survivors consolidate. Stability Field technology refined. Road networks established. |
| 50-100 | **The Purges.** Fear of planar-touched leads to mass executions. Restoration Cults gain power. |
| 100-150 | **The Reconciliation.** Threshold Cities open to planar-touched. Veil-Walkers organized as official messengers. |
| 150-200 | **The Expansion.** Exploration of Fractured Wilds. Discovery of planar-infused resources. |
| 200-250 | **The Concordat Wars.** Threshold Cities fight for dominance. Eventually form the Threshold Concordat. |
| 250-300 | **The Current Era.** Uneasy peace. Rising tensions. Whispers that the Veil is tearing further. |
| 312 (Now) | **The campaign begins.** Something is changing. The Seven Echoes are active again. |

## The State of the World

### Technology & Magic
- **Magitech** combines artifice with planar energy
- **Stability Engines** power Threshold Cities
- **Veil-Touched Items** are powerful but unpredictable
- **Communication** relies on messenger networks (Veil-Walkers) or expensive sending stones

### Economy
- **Essence Crystals** (solidified planar energy) are the primary currency
- **Stability** itself is a commodity—the wealthy live at city centers where fields are strongest
- **Information** is extremely valuable, as maps and knowledge become outdated quickly

### Religion
- Traditional gods are worshipped but questioned ("Where were they when the Veil tore?")
- New cults worship the Seven Echoes, planar entities, or the Shattering itself
- Many practice **pragmatic spirituality**—whatever works to survive

### Culture
- **Art** often reflects fragmentation, using mosaic styles, multiple perspectives, or reality-bending techniques
- **Music** incorporates "dissonant harmonies" that echo planar bleeding
- **Literature** favors survival stories, philosophical treatises on identity, and cautionary tales about hubris
- **Fashion** in Threshold Cities emphasizes protection and status; in the Wilds, practicality and personal expression

## Campaign Hooks

The world is poised for change:

- The **Seven Echoes** have begun appearing to mortals again, delivering cryptic prophecies
- The **Stability Fields** are showing signs of strain—some flicker, some shrink
- A **new plane** has begun bleeding through, one never documented before
- The **Maw** at The Crux is growing, its storms reaching further
- Someone claims to have found a way to **repair the Veil**—or to tear it completely

*The characters will decide which future unfolds.*

# Discovery Call Script (20 Minutes)

## Goal
Transition from curiosity to commitment — book the demo/show the dashboard.

## Pre-Call Prep (2 min)
- Check their site, recent ads, estimated revenue range
- Note any obvious pain points (heavy discounting, complex product mix, etc.)

## Opening (2 min)
"Thanks for jumping on. I know 20 minutes is tight, so I'll keep this moving. Quick context: I built Rior after running into this exact problem — scaling ads based on ROAS numbers that didn't reflect reality. Nearly tanked my own brand because of it.

So I built what I wished I had: a dashboard that shows actual net profit, not Shopify's fantasy math."

## Discovery Questions (8 min)

**Current State:**
1. "Walk me through how you're tracking profit right now — what tools, what reports?"
2. "How confident are you in your break-even ROAS? How do you calculate it?"
3. "When you look at last month's numbers, how long did it take to know if you were actually profitable?"

**Pain Points:**
4. "Ever had a month where Shopify said you crushed it, but your bank account told a different story?"
5. "How do you decide which campaigns to scale vs. kill? What's the actual data behind that decision?"
6. "What's your biggest blind spot right now — the thing you wish you could see but can't?"

**Impact:**
7. "If you had real-time profit visibility, what would change in how you run the business?"
8. "Rough ballpark — how much do you think you might be leaving on the table not seeing true numbers?"

## The Pivot (3 min)
"Here's what I'm hearing — [summarize their pain points].

That's exactly what we fix. Real profit numbers, updated daily, no spreadsheet gymnastics.

I can show you the dashboard right now — takes 5 minutes, and you'll see exactly how this works with real data. Want to take a look?"

## If They Say Yes (5 min)
- Screenshare the dashboard
- Show profit by channel, product, campaign
- Walk through break-even ROAS calculation
- Show where hidden costs show up

**Close:**
"This is what your numbers would look like. Setup takes 2-3 weeks. We're looking at $2,500-$4,000 to build it out, then $1,500-$3,000/month depending on complexity.

Does this solve what you're dealing with?"

## If They Say No (Time Constraints)
"Totally get it. How about this — I'll send you a 2-minute Loom walkthrough of a similar brand's dashboard. You can watch on your own time. If it clicks, we'll find 15 minutes next week. Fair?"

# Objection Handling Guide

## 1. "We already use Triple Whale / Northbeam / Lifetimely"

**Acknowledge:**
"Triple Whale's solid — lots of brands swear by it."

**Reframe:**
"Here's the gap: Triple Whale gives you blended metrics and attribution. It doesn't give you true net profit.

It estimates based on AOV and default margins. It doesn't pull actual COGS from your supplier invoices. It doesn't account for shipping variances. It doesn't factor payment processor fees in real-time.

So you get directionally accurate data. We give you bank-balance-accurate data. There's a difference."

**Pivot:**
"When's the last time you reconciled Triple Whale's profit number against your actual bank statement? Usually off by 15-30%."

---

## 2. "That's too expensive"

**Acknowledge:**
"Fair. It's not cheap."

**Reframe:**
"Let me ask this — how much did you spend on ads last month? [Wait for answer]

If you're off by even 10% on break-even ROAS, how much did that cost you? [Let them calculate]

If we prevent one bad scaling decision, the system pays for itself for a year."

**Pivot:**
"The question isn't whether you can afford $2,500-$4,000 setup. It's whether you can afford to keep making decisions without seeing true profit."

**Alternative:**
"If budget's tight, we can start with a lighter setup at $2,500 and expand once you see ROI. Sound fair?"

---

## 3. "We already have reporting in place"

**Acknowledge:**
"Got it — what does your current stack look like?"

**Probe:**
"How long does month-end close take you? How confident are you in the profit number the day after month-end? What about mid-month — can you see true profit by campaign while it's still running?"

**Reframe:**
"Most 'reporting' shows revenue and estimated margins. What it doesn't show is actual profit after every cost hits — including the ones that show up 30 days later.

If you're waiting until month-end to know if you were profitable, you're already too late to fix it."

**Pivot:**
"I'm not trying to replace your reporting. I'm trying to give you something none of your current tools can: real-time visibility into actual net profit. Want to see the difference?"

---

## 4. "I need to think about it / talk to my team"

**Acknowledge:**
"Totally — smart to get buy-in."

**Probe:**
"What specifically do you need to think through? Is it the investment, the timing, or something about the system itself?"

**Reframe (if investment):**
"Makes sense. What would need to be true for this to be an easy yes?"

**Reframe (if timing):**
"I get timing concerns. Honest question — what's going to change in 2 weeks or a month that makes this easier? Or is the real concern something else?"

**Pivot:**
"How about this: I'll send over a 3-minute Loom showing a similar brand's dashboard. You can share it with your team, and if it resonates, we'll find 15 minutes next week. No pressure either way."

**Last Resort:**
"I don't want to be pushy, but I also don't want you to make another scaling decision blind. What's one thing that would make this a no-brainer?"

---

## 5. "Can you send me more info?"

**Response:**
"I could, but honestly — the value is in seeing actual numbers move in real-time. A PDF or one-pager won't show you that.

What if I record a 3-minute Loom walkthrough of a similar brand's dashboard? You'll see exactly how this works without us needing to sync schedules."

**If they push for materials:**
"Sure — I'll send a one-pager, but fair warning: it's just words. The magic is in the live data. Watch the Loom first, then decide if you want the PDF. Deal?"

---

## 6. "We're not spending enough on ads to justify this"

**Acknowledge:**
"Got it — where are you at monthly on ad spend?"

**Reframe:**
"The threshold isn't about spend — it's about decision volume. If you're making daily calls on which campaigns to scale or kill, visibility matters.

But if you're spending $5k/month and barely touching campaigns, you're right — this might be overkill."

**Pivot:**
"What are you spending monthly, roughly? If it's under $20k, you might not need this yet."

---

## 7. "We just hired a new agency / changed platforms"

**Acknowledge:**
"Makes sense — lot of moving parts right now."

**Probe:**
"Is the new agency giving you profit visibility by campaign, or just ROAS numbers from the ad platforms?"

**Reframe:**
"Agencies optimize for their metrics — usually ROAS or MER. Those matter, but they're not profit. Most agencies don't have access to your actual cost data, so they're flying blind too.

This gives you — and them — the real picture."

**Pivot:**
"Fair to park this until the transition settles. Want me to check back in 30 days once you've got a baseline with the new setup?"
